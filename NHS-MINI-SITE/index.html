Out The Other Side

As most of you know, I've been working harder than ever and designed something called, OTOS Continuity™. It's a web-based connection layer that keeps a person visible between services, appointments, referrals and importantly - waiting lists.
I built it because I finally understand what happened to me — and I do not believe I am the only one.

Something was never right. I now believe my addiction to alcohol was a direct result of not being medicated properly for ADHD. For years, alcohol worked like medication. It gave me stimulation, relief, confidence and regulation — until it became dependence, crisis, rehab, relapse, loss and shame.

Once my ADHD was correctly reassessed and treated, my life changed within hours. The chaos stopped. My recovery became stable. My capacity returned. For the first time, I felt whole. I am convinced mine was not simply trauma-informed addiction. It was neurodiversely informed addiction — and when the ADHD was treated, the trajectory changed.

The system did not fail because nobody cared. It failed because nobody could see my whole journey, connect the pattern and intervene early enough around the real diagnosis. Too much was spent managing the addiction symptoms and the fallout, while the core ADHD condition stayed unresolved.

This private site is my starting point and support pack for a 12-week pre-pilot demonstrator in Cambridgeshire and Peterborough with 50 adults with co-occurring ADHD and alcohol and/or substance misuse. I need the people who saw this journey to help me take a bounded OTOS Continuity™ demonstrator to Local Authority, ICB and funder conversations — to prove that this ADHD + addiction cohort needs a priority route, not another blind wait.

Why this exists

Nobody held me in the space between them.
That empty space is where relapse begins.

The ADHD waiting list is the starting point of this story, but it is not the whole story. If my ADHD had been recognised, treated and held properly earlier, I do not believe alcohol would have become the system-level problem it later became.

That is the preventable second battle: people first wait with unmanaged ADHD, then reappear later in addiction, crisis, detox, rehab, hospital, housing pressure, police contact, mental health support or total collapse. By then, the original need has become much more expensive and much harder to unwind.

OTOS Continuity™ is built for the gaps: the space between discharge, waiting lists, referrals, recovery appointments, community support and the next real contact. It does not replace services or merge clinical records. It helps the same journey stay visible across the touchpoints where people usually slip.

For this ADHD and addiction cohort, every missed handoff matters. Waiting is not neutral when someone is using alcohol or drugs to survive untreated ADHD. The thread needs to hold long enough for the right diagnosis, the right pathway and the right support to meet the same person at the same time.

So the first version is simple: identify the person, connect the next step, keep the thread visible, and show where the pathway is working or failing.

The plan

12 weeks. 50 people.
One clear question.

This is not a request to prepare a full rollout. It is a request to answer a clear question: does a receipted continuity thread, applied to this cohort, measurably reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step?

01 — Identify

Find the cohort already visible

A bounded group of around 50 adults in Cambridgeshire and Peterborough who are already touching addiction, recovery, crisis, ADHD, mental health or community services — where unresolved ADHD may be driving relapse, repeat contact or waiting-list drift.

02 — Consent

Start with a plain-English ask

A named keyworker introduces OTOS as a continuity check-in that stays with the person between services. Consent is recorded, the person is onboarded with a tokenised journey ID, and the continuity thread begins.

03 — Hold

Keep the thread alive

OTOS logs handoffs, monitors continuity touchpoints, generates transition receipts and surfaces early drift signals for human review. It does not diagnose, prescribe, replace clinical judgement or own the clinical record.

04 — Prove

Show what changes

The pre-pilot measures whether silent disengagement becomes visible earlier, whether people reach the next meaningful step, how much partner time is required, and whether this cohort has a case for a priority route.

What is being tested

This is a pre-pilot, not a treatment programme. It does not aim to diagnose, prescribe or replace any clinical function. Its purpose is to generate locally validated evidence that a continuity mechanism works before any larger-scale rollout is considered.

The ideal starting point is a carefully selected cohort where ADHD and addiction are already both visible, including people who are diagnosed, suspected, waiting, stuck between steps or already stabilised enough to provide clean continuity evidence quickly. The exact inclusion criteria should be agreed with CPFT, CGL, CRS, SUN Network and local commissioners before launch.

How it works

Continuity, not control.
A thread between services.

OTOS Continuity™ is a non-clinical continuity layer. It strengthens the thread between services without taking over clinical decisions, replacing systems or creating another medical record.

Touchpoints

Receipted handoffs

When a person moves between services, the handoff is logged and receipted. The point is simple: someone can see whether the next step happened, stalled or disappeared.

Signals

Early drift made visible

Missed responses, broken contact, delayed next steps and disengagement signals become visible earlier — before they become relapse, crisis, detox, hospital or another lost month.

Human review

No autonomous clinical action

Every drift signal requires a human review decision before action. OTOS does not diagnose, prescribe, escalate clinically on its own or tell a service what decision to make.

Evidence

A local proof point

The demonstrator shows where journeys stall, where drop-off happens, what partner burden looks like, and whether continuity support changes the pathway enough to justify the next phase.
