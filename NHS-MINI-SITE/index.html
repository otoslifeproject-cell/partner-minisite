From Dean — directly to you

You helped me.
Now let me help you.

This is a private briefing for the people who helped me through the system — and who I promised I would return with something that could help the next person get there faster and with less damage.

Services exist. Staff exist. Pathways exist on paper. But for the ADHD + addiction overlap cohort, an ADHD‑aware pathway or continuity infrastructure to keep the whole journey visible is missing — so people are told there is a pathway, but in reality they are moving across handoffs, silence and drift while ADHD is left waiting and addiction risk rises.

The ADHD waiting list is the starting point of this story, but it is not the whole story. If my ADHD had been recognised, treated, and held properly earlier, I do not believe alcohol would have become the system-level problem it later became.
That is the preventable second battle: people first wait with unmanaged ADHD, then reappear later in A&E, addiction, crisis, therapy, detox, rehab, hospital, housing pressure, police contact, mental health support, total collapse — and sadly on to jail or suicide. By then, the original need has become much more expensive and much harder to unwind.

OTOS Continuity™ is a web-based continuity layer that targets ADHD-driven addiction, whilst keeping the person visible between CPFT & CGL services, referrals, waiting lists and the next meaningful step — so partners can see where the journey is holding, where it is slipping, and where a human follow-up could prevent relapse, hospital, detox, rehab or another expensive crisis.
