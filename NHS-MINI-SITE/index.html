Out The Other Side

There was no pathway for me

I had to create my own route to survive. Now I’ve designed the solution.

OTOS Continuity™ is a web-based continuity layer that targets ADHD-driven addiction, whilst keeping a person visible between mental health and addiction services, referrals, waiting lists and the next meaningful step — so partners can see where the journey is holding, where it is slipping, and where a human follow-up could prevent relapse, hospital, detox, rehab or another expensive crisis.

This private site is my starting point and support pack for a targeted 12-week, 50-person pre-pilot demonstrator in Cambridgeshire and Peterborough. I need the people who saw this journey to help me take it into ICB and funder conversations — to prove this ADHD + addiction cohort needs a priority route, not another blind wait.

Each partner page includes a short draft note you can edit and send back to me or arrange a quick call. Please read your page first, then feel free to click around and explore.

Why this exists

Nobody held me in the space between them.
That empty space is where relapse begins.

The ADHD waiting list is the starting point of this story, but it is not the whole story. If my ADHD had been recognised, treated and held properly earlier, I do not believe alcohol would have become the system-level problem it later became.

That is the preventable second battle: people first wait with unmanaged ADHD, then reappear later in addiction, crisis, detox, rehab, hospital, housing pressure, police contact, mental health support or total collapse. By then, the original need has become much more expensive and much harder to unwind.

OTOS Continuity™ is built for the gaps: the space between discharge, waiting lists, referrals, recovery appointments, community support and the next real contact. It does not replace services or merge clinical records. It helps the same journey stay visible across the touchpoints where people usually slip.

For this ADHD and addiction cohort, every missed handoff matters. Waiting is not neutral when someone is using alcohol or drugs to survive untreated ADHD. The thread needs to hold long enough for the right diagnosis, the right pathway and the right support to meet the same person at the same time.

So the first version is simple: identify the person, connect the next step, keep the thread visible, and show where the pathway is working or failing.

The plan

12 weeks. 50 people.
One clear question.

This is not a request to prepare a full rollout. It is a request to answer a clear question: does a receipted continuity thread, applied to this cohort, measurably reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step?

01 — Identify

Find the cohort already visible

A bounded group of around 50 adults in Cambridgeshire and Peterborough who are already touching addiction, recovery, crisis, ADHD, mental health or community services — where unresolved ADHD may be driving relapse, repeat contact or waiting-list drift.

02 — Consent

Start with a plain-English ask

A named keyworker introduces OTOS as a continuity check-in that stays with the person between services. Consent is recorded, the person is onboarded with a tokenised journey ID, and the continuity thread begins.

03 — Hold

Keep the thread alive

OTOS logs handoffs, monitors continuity touchpoints, generates transition receipts and surfaces early drift signals for human review. It does not diagnose, prescribe, replace clinical judgement or own the clinical record.

04 — Prove

Show what changes

The pre-pilot measures whether silent disengagement becomes visible earlier, whether people reach the next meaningful step, how much partner time is required, and whether this cohort has a case for a priority route.

How it works

Continuity, not control.
A thread between services.

OTOS Continuity™ is a non-clinical continuity layer. It strengthens the thread between services without taking over clinical decisions, replacing systems or creating another medical record.

Touchpoints

Receipted handoffs

When a person moves between services, the handoff is logged and receipted. Someone can see whether the next step happened, stalled or disappeared.

Signals

Early drift made visible

Missed responses, broken contact, delayed next steps and disengagement signals become visible earlier — before they become relapse, crisis, detox or hospital.

Human review

No autonomous clinical action

Every drift signal requires a human review decision before action. OTOS does not diagnose, prescribe, escalate clinically on its own or tell a service what decision to make.

Evidence

A local proof point

The demonstrator shows where journeys stall, where drop-off happens, what partner burden looks like, and whether continuity support justifies the next phase.

What it looks like in practice

One person.
One continuous pathway.

The person stays visible as they move between referral, waiting list, check-in, discharge, community support and next contact. Not one merged clinical record. Not another service. A continuity view that shows where the journey is holding — and where it is starting to break.
