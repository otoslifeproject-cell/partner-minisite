The problem

The system sees addiction first.
The driver may be ADHD.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

That matters because self-medication can work — until it does not. Alcohol or substances can briefly do what an untreated ADHD brain has been trying to do all along: supply motivation, sustain attention, create focus, cut the noise. The trouble starts when that coping strategy turns into dependency and crisis.

At that point, services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work often does not hold. Support can fail to land when untreated ADHD is still disrupting sequencing, follow-through, memory and emotional regulation.

“The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view. The ADHD is what the pathway still has not reached.”
What OTOS is

Not a new service.
The continuity infrastructure around the route.

OTOS Continuity™ does not replace CGL, CRS, CPFT, GPs, NHS ADHD services, hospitals, VCSE partners or community support. It connects the route between them, so the person’s next step is visible, receipted and held.

What it creates

  • Handoffs become visible and receipted instead of assumed.
  • Waiting-list silence becomes a signal, not a blind spot.
  • Micro-check-ins create lightweight engagement signals without requiring a keyworker phone call.
  • Partners can send familiar, governed messages in their own voice where agreed.
  • Human review closes the loop before any action is taken.

What it is not

  • Not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • Not a prescribing, treatment or clinical decision-support system.
  • Not surveillance, and not full record-sharing by default.
  • Not another service asking frontline teams to start again.
  • Not a replacement for safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.
How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS Continuity™ holds the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status, not another service’s clinical notes.

01

Identify

People already touching addiction, recovery, crisis, hospital, GP, ADHD or community support are identified where co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse may be clinically relevant.

02

Connect

The next appropriate route is named: ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, recovery support, mental health or community support. The handoff is logged.

03

Co-ordinate

The continuity thread shows whether the handoff landed, stalled or needs human review. No extra clinical record is shared.

04

Sustain

Drift, silence and missed follow-through become visible early enough for partner-led review, safe re-engagement and measurable learning.

The pre-pilot

50 people.
A surgical proof point.

The Cambridge pre-pilot is a bounded demonstrator for a hand-picked, high-cost cohort: adults moving through addiction, crisis, recovery and fragmented support where unresolved ADHD may be keeping the pattern alive.

The aim is not to ask the system to change overnight. It is to test whether partners can identify this cohort, activate the right ADHD-related step faster, and use OTOS Continuity™ to keep the journey visible enough that people do not disappear between services.

The cost of doing nothing

The tsunami is building.
Nobody can see it building.

Adults with undiagnosed or unresolved ADHD can cycle through A&E, housing, probation, detox and recovery support while the pathway keeps treating the symptom that arrives first.

If adult ADHD remains out of reach, the cost does not disappear. It moves into addiction, crisis, homelessness, repeat presentation and preventable partner workload.

“OTOS surfaces signals. The partner team makes the decision.”
