What OTOS is

The continuity layer.
One safe signal through every service.

OTOS is non-clinical continuity infrastructure. It creates an encrypted, tokenised thread around a person’s journey, so separate services can see whether a handoff landed, whether the next step happened, and whether someone has gone quiet.

It does not merge records. It does not share clinical notes. It does not diagnose, prescribe, triage clinically or replace existing systems. It sits around them — surfacing continuity signals so partner teams can make better, faster, human decisions.

What it does

  • Logs touchpoints and handoffs against an anonymised journey token.
  • Creates timestamped transition receipts when a next step is made, acknowledged or stalled.
  • Surfaces early drift signals for human review before silence becomes crisis.
  • Supports governed message delivery and micro-check-ins where partners agree the content and route.
  • Maintains a continuity audit trail that can be reviewed, measured and improved.

What it is not

  • Not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • Not a prescribing, treatment or clinical decision-support system.
  • Not surveillance, and not full record-sharing by default.
  • Not another service asking frontline teams to start again.
  • Not a replacement for safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.
The problem

Hidden in plain sight.
ADHD is driving addiction.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

That matters because self-medication can work — until it does not. Alcohol or substances can briefly do what an untreated ADHD brain has been trying to do all along: supply motivation, sustain attention, create focus and cut the noise. The trouble starts when that coping strategy turns into dependency and crisis.

How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS does not replace existing services. It connects them and maintains the person’s thread through every organisation they touch. The person can remain anonymous. The clinical notes stay where they are. The continuity signal travels.

1

Identify

Screen and identify people in addiction, recovery, crisis or waiting-list pathways who show indicators of co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse. OTOS does not diagnose — it surfaces the signal.

2

Connect

Warm-transfer people to appropriate NHS and partner routes — ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, mental health support or recovery support. The receipt is logged. The handoff becomes visible.

3

Co-ordinate

Maintain a tokenised continuity thread across services so each partner can see where the person is in the journey — reducing gaps, duplication and silent disengagement before it becomes crisis.

4

Sustain

Support follow-through, recovery and safe re-engagement. Micro-check-ins can create simple “signal of life” touchpoints and habit-stacked repetitions while partners keep control of messaging, escalation and decisions.

OTOS surfaces signals. The partner team makes the decision.
The pre-pilot

50 people.
A proof point that changes the commissioning conversation.

The pre-pilot is a bounded, tactical intervention: select a high-priority cohort, hold the continuity thread across existing services, and test whether visible, receipted handoffs reduce drift and improve follow-through.

Pilot ambition: for a defined cohort of up to 50, help people progress through assessment and, where clinically indicated, onto treatment including medication — using existing routes under clinical leadership. OTOS connects them to existing support, holds the pathway visible, and surfaces when continuity breaks.

Adults with co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse, selected with partner input. Diagnosed, suspected, waiting or stuck between steps can all be considered.

A small amount of time now so the future system reduces burden later. The point is to make the pathway easier, not heavier.
