Out The Other Side

This happened
to me.

OTOS Continuity™ was built from a shift — not from theory, but from lived experience and the belief that the next person should not need that much damage before the right condition is finally reached.

For years, the underlying condition was there, but it was not properly recognised or treated. What followed was chaos, crisis, shame, addiction, court, probation and repeated contact with services that were dealing with the visible damage while the deeper cause remained unresolved.

"Within hours, my whole life story made sense. The chaos, the addiction, the hospital admissions, probation, shame, survival patterns and every failed attempt to explain myself suddenly had a frame."

I threw my communications experience into the mix and started marrying everything up — lived experience, pathway failures, timing gaps, silence, drift, recovery, ADHD and the bigger picture across the local system.

As the ideas distilled, the answer became Continuity. Not because I thought I could rebuild everything, but because I could see exactly where the system had served me wrong: the lack of communication between services, the lack of visibility between touchpoints, and the lack of anyone holding the thread while the right intervention was still unresolved.

The point where the right treatment begins to hold.
NIA / The problem

The system saw addiction.
I saw untreated ADHD.

Services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work often does not hold. The relapse was not proof that treatment failed — it was proof that the right condition was never properly treated in the first place.

Neuro-Informed Addiction

Addiction in the foreground.
Untreated ADHD beneath.

Alcohol or substances can briefly do what an untreated ADHD brain has been trying to do all along: supply motivation, sustain attention, create focus and cut the noise. Then the coping strategy turns into dependency, crisis, shame, suicide risk, court, probation, relapse and repeated cost.

At that point, services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work often does not hold. Support can fail to land when untreated ADHD is still disrupting sequencing, memory, follow-through, motivation and emotional regulation.

Trauma-informed care treats the story. Neuro-informed care stabilises the brain. Recovery needs both.

"I did recovery groups, therapy, meetings, detox, relapse prevention — all of it helped. But only when my ADHD was treated did any of it finally work."

Why NIA belongs here

The term helps describe a pattern the system can miss: addiction in the foreground, unresolved ADHD in the background, and a person moving through services without the right condition being stabilised early enough. Not a replacement for trauma-informed care — a sharper description of a cohort that may need both.

What OTOS is making visible

Not every addiction story is ADHD. But for a real, high-cost, high-risk cohort, the system may be treating the visible damage while the deeper cause remains unresolved. This first phase is deliberately small: diagnose, medicate, place this cohort on OTOS Continuity™, then measure what changes.

●  What OTOS is

Not a new service.
A continuity layer around the services already there.

OTOS does not replace existing services. It connects them and maintains the person's thread through every organisation they touch. The person can remain anonymous. The clinical notes stay where they are. The continuity signal travels.

What it creates
  • ✓Referrals and next steps become visible and receipted instead of assumed.
  • ✓Waiting-list silence becomes a continuity signal, not a blind spot.
  • ✓Micro-check-ins create lightweight engagement signals without requiring a keyworker phone call.
  • ✓Partners can send familiar, governed messages in their own voice where agreed.
  • ✓Human review closes the loop before any action is taken.
What it is not
  • ×Not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • ×Not a prescribing, treatment or clinical decision-support system.
  • ×Not surveillance, and not full record-sharing by default.
  • ×Not another service asking frontline teams to start again.
  • ×Not a replacement for safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.
How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS Continuity™ holds the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status — not another service's clinical notes.

01

Identify

People already touching addiction, recovery, crisis, hospital, GP, ADHD or community support are identified where co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse may be clinically relevant.

02

Connect

The next appropriate route is named: ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, recovery or community support. The referral is logged and the next step is visible.

03

Co-ordinate

The continuity thread shows whether the next step landed, stalled or needs human review. No extra clinical record is shared between services.

04

Sustain

Drift, silence and missed follow-through become visible early enough for partner-led review, safe re-engagement and measurable learning.

Hospital to community

Where discharge meets follow-through.

The 10 Year Health Plan direction is hospital to community, analogue to digital, reactive to preventive. OTOS tests that transition safely — starting at the earliest possible point of contact with services.

So this first phase is deliberately small. One survivor with an idea, trying to prove the simplest useful version first — diagnose, medicate, place this cohort on OTOS Continuity™, then measure what changes.
