When ADHD is left waiting, addiction steps in. And it doesn’t come quietly.

This is a private briefing for NHS, local authority, addiction, recovery, mental-health and community partners in Cambridge and Peterborough.

It is about a targeted ADHD + addiction continuity intervention: a 12-week, 50-person first phase for adults already visible to services, where unresolved ADHD may be driving repeated crisis, relapse, disengagement and cost.

OTOS Continuity™ is not diagnosis, not prescribing, and not a new treatment service. It is a secure continuity layer around existing services — keeping referrals, appointment prompts, touchpoints, micro-check-ins, continuity signals and review flags visible while the right intervention is still in motion.

I believe my addiction was a result of not being medicated for ADHD. For years, the underlying condition was there, but it was not properly recognised or treated. What followed was chaos, crisis, shame, addiction, court, probation and repeated contact with services that were dealing with the visible damage while the deeper cause remained unresolved.

Within hours, my whole life story made sense. The chaos, the addiction, the hospital admissions, probation, shame, survival patterns and every failed attempt to explain myself suddenly had a frame.

This happened to me.

OTOS Continuity™ was built from that shift — not from theory, but from lived experience and the belief that the next person should not need that much damage before the right condition is finally reached.

Looking back, it was not wasted time. It was market research — brutal, personal, expensive market research — preparing me to build Out The Other Side.

Why this exists

I started by trying to build a recovery platform.
Then I realised I needed to build continuity.

I threw my communications experience into the mix and started marrying everything up — lived experience, pathway failures, timing gaps, silence, drift, recovery, ADHD and the bigger picture across the local system.

As the ideas distilled, the answer became Continuity. Not because I thought I could rebuild everything, but because I could see exactly where the system had served me wrong: the lack of communication between services, the lack of visibility between touchpoints, and the lack of anyone holding the thread while the right intervention was still unresolved.

So this first phase is deliberately small. I am one survivor with an idea, trying to prove the simplest useful version first — diagnose, medicate and place this cohort on OTOS Continuity™, then measure what changes.

That is why the next section matters: the system usually responds to the addiction first, even when ADHD may be the condition driving the pattern underneath it.
The problem

The system sees addiction first.
The driver may be ADHD.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

That matters because self-medication can work — until it does not. Alcohol or substances can briefly do what an untreated ADHD brain has been trying to do all along: supply motivation, sustain attention, create focus and cut the noise. The trouble starts when that coping strategy turns into dependency and crisis.

At that point, services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work often does not hold. Support can fail to land when untreated ADHD is still disrupting sequencing, memory, follow-through, motivation and emotional regulation.

The relapse was not proof that treatment failed. It was proof that the right condition was never properly treated in the first place.
What OTOS is

There is no joined-up pathway.
So we are building the continuity layer.

Services exist. Buildings exist. Staff exist. Pathways exist on paper. But the connection layer was never built. OTOS Continuity™ helps existing services by making the pathway visible between them.

What it creates

  • Referrals and next steps become visible and receipted instead of assumed.
  • Waiting-list silence becomes a continuity signal, not a blind spot.
  • Micro-check-ins create lightweight engagement signals without requiring a keyworker phone call.
  • Partners can send familiar, governed messages in their own voice where agreed.
  • Human review closes the loop before action is taken.

What it is not

  • Not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • Not a prescribing, treatment or clinical decision-support system.
  • Not surveillance, and not full record-sharing by default.
  • Not another service asking frontline teams to start again.
  • Not a replacement for safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.
How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS Continuity™ holds the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status, not another service’s clinical notes.

01

Identify

People already touching addiction, recovery, crisis, hospital, GP, ADHD or community support are identified where co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse may be clinically relevant.

02

Connect

The next appropriate route is named: ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, recovery support, mental health or community support. The referral is logged and the next step is visible.

03

Co-ordinate

The continuity thread shows whether the next step landed, stalled or needs human review. No extra clinical record is shared.

04

Sustain

Drift, silence and missed follow-through become visible early enough for partner-led review, safe re-engagement and measurable learning.

Hospital to community

When someone is at their lowest, OTOS is already there.

Not as a clinical service. As the continuity thread: the referral, receipt, prompt and next-step signal that can remain visible when the person moves between services.
