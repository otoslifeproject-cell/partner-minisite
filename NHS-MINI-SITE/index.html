What OTOS is

Continuity infrastructure.
With message-delivery and micro-check-in capability.

OTOS does not replace services. It sits around them. It creates a tokenised continuity thread that helps authorised partners see whether a touchpoint happened, whether a handoff landed, whether a person is still engaging, and whether silence is becoming drift.

Partners can approve short, familiar messages for their own touchpoints — for example a CGL welcome message, an RCE course nudge while someone waits, or a discharge follow-through confirmation. OTOS provides the infrastructure; the partner decides the wording, timing and responsibility.

OTOS does not deliver care directly to patients. It gives partner teams the tools to hold the pathway, receipt handoffs, send agreed messages, and see when continuity is starting to break.

The problem

ADHD and addiction do not fail in one service.
They fail between them.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

Services then respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work often does not hold. Motivation strategies meet an executive function problem. Recovery planning meets a brain that struggles with sequencing, memory, time, shame and follow-through.

23–45%
ADHD in addiction treatment
Published research shows ADHD is significantly overrepresented in substance-use populations. The exact figure varies by setting and definition.
£1.3bn
Annual NHS addiction service spend
Much of the cost is reactive. The public-health question is whether some of this spend can be redirected earlier, before repeat crisis.
800–900
Estimated local risk cohort
OTOS modelling estimates between 800 and 900 adults in the Cambridgeshire and Peterborough footprint may sit within the target ADHD-and-addiction risk profile. This is a planning estimate, not a CPFT-reported figure.
A system can appear to succeed when a person is discharged, closed, referred on, or marked as having completed an episode. But if nobody can see whether the next step actually happened, the risk has not disappeared — it has simply moved out of view.
How it works

Existing services. Existing routes.
One continuity layer.

OTOS sits lightly around existing workflows. No merged records. No clinical decision-making. No new clinical burden. The system creates a continuity thread, records touchpoints, receipts handoffs, and surfaces drift for human review.

1
Identify
A partner identifies someone already touching addiction, mental health, hospital, recovery, GP or community support where ADHD may be driving repeat crisis or disengagement.
2
Connect
The person is connected to the next appropriate route — ADHD assessment, medication review, RCE support, CGL/CRS follow-through, GP/RTC/QbTest route or community support — under partner governance.
3
Hold the thread
A tokenised journey thread records the touchpoint and expected next step. Clinical records stay where they are. OTOS holds continuity status, not full clinical notes.
4
Surface drift
If the handoff is not receipted, the check-in goes quiet, or the pathway starts to break, OTOS surfaces a drift signal for the named human reviewer.
5
Partner team decides
OTOS does not diagnose, prescribe, triage clinically, or decide care. The partner team decides what happens next. OTOS makes the continuity picture visible.

The pre-pilot

50 people.
A proof point that changes the commissioning conversation.

The pre-pilot is a tactical, surgical intervention first. It targets a bounded, high-cost cohort already touching multiple services, then tests whether visible, receipted continuity changes what happens next.

This is not a request to fund a full rollout. It is a request to fund a clear question: does a receipted continuity thread, applied to this cohort, measurably reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step?
