Out The Other Side

I’ve designed a web-based system to help you, the NHS and people like me.
People like me with what I now think of as neurodiversely informed addiction.

Something was never right. I now believe my addiction to alcohol was a direct result of not being medicated properly for ADHD. And I am not alone in that. One clinical study found a 61.3% improvement in addictive disorders once ADHD was properly identified and treated.

I am convinced mine was not simply trauma-informed. Correct ADHD medication has literally changed my life and explained so much of it. Now, as promised, I am back with a plan. It is extensive, so I will let the pages written for each service do the talking — but please do click around.

The system did not fail because nobody cared. It failed because nobody could see my whole journey, connect the pattern and intervene early enough around the real diagnosis. Too much was spent managing the addiction symptoms and the fallout, while the core ADHD condition stayed unresolved.

That is why OTOS exists: to make the pattern visible earlier, before repeated crisis, repeated spend, repeated personal loss and system-wide cost become the story.

This private site is my starting point and support pack for a 12-week pre-pilot demonstrator in Cambridgeshire and Peterborough with 50 adults with co-occurring ADHD and alohol and or substance misuse. I need the people who saw this journey to help me take a bounded OTOS Continuity™ demonstrator to Local Authority, ICB and funder conversations — to prove that this ADHD + addiction cohort needs a priority route, not another blind wait.

Why this exists

Because the moment care pauses,
the risk does not.

At the points where someone is discharged, waiting, transitioning, or simply struggling with executive function, connection is lost, relapse risk rises and cost returns to the system.

OTOS Continuity™ exists to hold that gap. Not by replacing services. Not by taking over clinical decisions. Not by creating another record. It is the silent layer that helps the pathway stay visible while each service keeps doing its own work.

For this ADHD and addiction cohort, ordinary waiting can be dangerous. If the thread breaks, addiction fills the space again. OTOS is designed to show where that is happening early enough for a human to act.

The job is simple: identify the person, connect the next step, keep the thread visible, and show where the pathway is working or failing.

Why this exists

Nobody held me in the space between them.
That empty space is where relapse begins.

For this ADHD and addiction cohort, every missed handoff matters. Waiting is not neutral when someone is using alcohol or drugs to survive untreated ADHD. The thread needs to hold long enough for the right diagnosis, the right pathway and the right support to meet the same person at the same time.

OTOS Continuity™ is built for: the gaps, the space between discharge, waiting lists, referrals, recovery appointments, community support and the next real contact.

All the support already exists. It has simply never been connected like this. OTOS does not replace services or merge clinical records. It helps the same journey stay visible across the touchpoints where people usually slip.
