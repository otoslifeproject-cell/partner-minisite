The problem

Hidden in plain sight.
ADHD is driving addiction.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

At that point, services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work does not hold. CBT can be delivered to a brain that cannot sequence or retain it. Motivation strategies meet an executive function that was never the problem.

What OTOS is

One continuity layer.
Not another clinical system.

OTOS is non-clinical continuity infrastructure. It sits around existing services so the person’s journey can be held between handoffs, waiting lists, discharge points, recovery support and crisis risk.

What it helps create

  • Earlier visibility when follow-through fails.
  • Safer handoffs between separate services.
  • Less duplicated chasing and fewer invisible gaps.
  • Governed micro-check-ins and message delivery where partners agree the content and route.
  • A continuity audit trail that can be reviewed, measured and improved.

What it does not do

  • It does not diagnose, prescribe or make clinical decisions.
  • It does not replace EPRs, clinical records or partner systems.
  • It does not require full record sharing by default.
  • It does not add a new clinical burden or replace safeguarding protocols.
  • It surfaces signals. Partner teams decide.
How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS does not replace existing services. It connects them and maintains the person’s thread through every organisation they touch. No merged clinical notes. No EPR write-back. No diagnosis by OTOS. Just visible continuity, receipted handoffs and human review.

1

Identify

Screen and identify people in addiction, recovery, crisis or waiting-list pathways who show indicators of co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse. OTOS does not diagnose — it surfaces the signal.

2

Connect

Warm-transfer people to appropriate NHS and partner routes — ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, mental health support or recovery support. The receipt is logged. The handoff becomes visible.

3

Co-ordinate

Hold the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status, not another service’s clinical notes.

4

Sustain

Support appropriate follow-through, medication routes where clinically indicated, recovery support, safe re-engagement and measurable outcomes.

The pre-pilot

50 people.
A proof point that changes the commissioning conversation.

The pre-pilot is a bounded 12-week demonstrator. It does not ask the system to buy another silo. It asks whether a visible, receipted and governed continuity thread can reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step.
