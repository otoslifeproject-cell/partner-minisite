The problem

Hidden in plain sight.
ADHD is driving addiction.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

At that point, services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work does not hold. CBT is delivered to a brain that cannot sequence or retain it. Motivation strategies meet an executive function that was never the problem.

“Continuity is not a nice-to-have. It is where prevention must start.”
What OTOS is

Not a new service.
A continuity layer around the services already there.

OTOS does not replace existing services. It connects them and maintains the person’s thread through every organisation they touch. The person can remain anonymous. The clinical notes stay where they are. The continuity signal travels.

What it creates

  • Handoffs become visible and receipted instead of assumed.
  • Waiting-list silence becomes a signal, not a blind spot.
  • Micro-check-ins create a simple “sign of life” without requiring a keyworker phone call.
  • Partners can send familiar, governed messages in their own voice where agreed.
  • Human review closes the loop before any action is taken.

What it is not

  • Not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • Not a prescribing, treatment or clinical decision-support system.
  • Not surveillance, and not full record-sharing by default.
  • Not another service asking frontline teams to start again.
  • Not a replacement for safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.
How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS holds the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status, not another service’s clinical notes.

01

Identify

Screen people in addiction, recovery, crisis or waiting-list pathways who show indicators of co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse. OTOS surfaces the signal; it does not diagnose.

02

Connect

Warm-transfer people to the right NHS or partner route — ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, mental health or recovery support. The receipt is logged.

03

Co-ordinate

Hold the continuity thread across services so each partner can see whether the handoff landed, stalled or needs human review. No extra clinical record is shared.

04

Sustain

Support safe follow-through — medication routes where clinically indicated, recovery support, re-engagement and measurable outcomes across the pathway.

The pre-pilot

50 people.
A proof point that changes the commissioning conversation.

The 12-week Cambridge pre-pilot is a bounded demonstrator for a hand-picked, high-cost cohort: adults moving through addiction, crisis and fragmented support where unresolved ADHD may be driving repeat demand.

The aim is not to ask the system to change overnight. It is to test whether a receipted continuity thread, applied to this cohort, measurably reduces silent disengagement and increases the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step.

The cost of doing nothing

The tsunami is building.
Nobody can see it building.

Thousands of adults with undiagnosed ADHD are cycling through A&E, housing, probation, detox and recovery support. OTOS is the early warning layer — and the breakwater.

If services are forced to prioritise children and early intervention while adults over 23 wait or fall out of scope, the cost does not disappear. It moves into addiction, crisis, homelessness and repeat presentation.
