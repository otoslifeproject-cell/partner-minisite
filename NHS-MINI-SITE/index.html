From Dean — directly to you

This is a private briefing for the people who helped me through the system — and who I promised I would return with something that could help the next person get there faster and with less damage.

The ADHD waiting list is the starting point of this story, but it is not the whole story. If my ADHD had been recognised, treated and held properly earlier, I do not believe alcohol would have become the system-level problem it later became.

That is the preventable second battle: people first wait with unmanaged ADHD, then reappear later in A&E, addiction, crisis, therapy, detox, rehab, hospital, housing pressure, police contact, mental health support, total collapse, prison and worse. By then, the original need has become much more expensive and much harder to unwind.

OTOS Continuity™ is a web-based continuity layer that targets ADHD-driven addiction by keeping the person visible between CPFT & CGL services, referrals, waiting lists and the next meaningful step — so partners can see where the journey is holding, where it is slipping and where a human follow-up could prevent relapse, hospital, detox, rehab or another expensive crisis.

Same person. ADHD and addiction. Two silos. One continuity thread.

I’m proposing a fast-track route for this overlap cohort — not by creating a new clinical service, but by holding the thread long enough for the right thing to happen: identify people like me early, progress ADHD assessment/diagnosis and (where clinically indicated) medication, and then let addiction treatment actually land. That’s why Phase 1 is deliberately small: a surgical 12-week demonstrator for 50 people, designed to show whether receipted handoffs and early drift visibility stop people being lost while they wait.
