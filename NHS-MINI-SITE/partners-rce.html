What community holding looks like when the system has run out

The gap between services is where people get lost.

RCE was where I went when the clinical pathway had nothing left to offer immediately. I was referred, I was waiting, I had no keyworker and no contact from the ADHD team. RCE held me. Franceska ran the course. She got it immediately — the ADHD piece, the addiction piece, how they interact.

That kind of holding is not visible in any clinical record. It is not receipted anywhere. It exists in the memory of the person and the practitioner — and when either moves on, the continuity disappears with them.

OTOS makes that holding visible. Not by recording what happens in sessions — that stays private. But by making the thread legible: this person is engaged, here, with this support, as part of this wider pathway.

"Community support is often the only thing holding a person in the system during the wait. OTOS makes that visible — so the next service knows it happened."
What OTOS makes visible
  • Active community engagement during waiting periods
  • Thread between RCE and clinical services — receipted, not assumed
  • Continuity of support across transitions — so the next service isn't starting from zero
No NHS record access needed

RCE is a VCSE community partner. OTOS is designed specifically to work across VCSE and NHS contexts — without requiring NHS record access, system integration, or clinical governance infrastructure that community organisations don't have.

What OTOS needs from RCE

Three things. None of them clinical.

Named contact

One person at RCE

A named contact to help shape how OTOS connects with RCE's community support offer — and to confirm what RCE is and isn't comfortable with in terms of thread visibility.

Thread entry point

Engagement made visible

When someone engaging with RCE is part of the pre-pilot cohort, a lightweight signal that they're engaged — no session content, no clinical information, just a receipted thread entry.

Scoping conversation

Thirty minutes together

Map what RCE does, agree the limits of what OTOS makes visible, and establish a community-appropriate way of participating in the pre-pilot.

What OTOS will never do
  • ✕Access or share session content or personal support records
  • ✕Require NHS record access or clinical system integration
  • ✕Make clinical or safeguarding decisions — those stay with RCE
  • ✕Change how RCE community support is delivered
  • ✕Identify participants to other services without consent
Questions to resolve together
  • ?What is RCE comfortable making visible in the OTOS thread?
  • ?How does OTOS fit with RCE's existing data-sharing approach?
  • ?Who are the right people from RCE to be in the scoping call?
  • ?What consent model works for RCE's community context?
