From Dean — directly to RCE

You were the place I could go
when I didn't know where else to go.

Holding support while someone waits is not a small thing. It is often the difference between staying connected and drifting completely. OTOS makes that holding visible — and receipted.

What we need from RCE

Give people something real to engage with.
While continuity stays active.

RCE belongs in the pilot framing as a real, existing between-appointments support option while people are waiting or moving between services. Not passive waiting — active holding.

Bite-sized wellbeing support

Offer bite-sized wellbeing courses and practical support options that can appear as a real next step in OTOS prompts during waits and handoffs.

A safe destination

Provide a safe, real next-step destination that OTOS can point to — so the prompt isn't empty, and the person isn't left with nothing to do while they wait.

Shape the language

Contribute partner-shaped language for co-written messages. Your voice, your tone, your service — not a generic system message pretending to be from you.

In OTOS terms, RCE is not a clinical decision-maker and not a pathway owner. It is one of the best existing places the continuity thread can point to while people wait.
A credible holding layer

Not passive waiting.
Active continuity.

Between appointments

RCE Wellbeing Hub belongs in the pilot as a real, existing between-appointments option. A course. A session. A genuine next step that isn't just "wait for your appointment."

Co-written prompts

Co-written, non-clinical prompts can point to a real course, session or next step. A reply or interaction becomes a logged continuity signal on the person's journey.

No clinical responsibility added

RCE is not being asked to make clinical decisions. It is being asked to be a real, trusted next step in the continuity thread — which it already is in practice.

Clear boundaries

What OTOS will never do.
And what stays entirely with you.

Guardrails — always

  • No new clinical record to maintain.
  • No diagnosis, prescribing or clinical decision-making by OTOS.
  • No EPR write-back.
  • No commitment to a full rollout by joining the scoping conversation.

Questions to resolve together

  • Which courses or sessions work best as continuity next steps?
  • What wording feels right for messages sent in your name?
  • What interaction signals are appropriate to log?
  • Who needs to be in the scoping conversation?
