What we need from RCE

Not passive waiting.
Active, receipted holding.

RCE belongs in the pilot as a real, existing between-appointments option. While someone waits for CPFT or CGL, OTOS can point them to your Wednesday morning group. When they respond to the prompt, that becomes a logged continuity signal on their journey.

A real next step

Offer a real, practical destination that can appear in OTOS prompts — a session, a course, a conversation. Something real that someone can actually do while they wait for clinical next steps.

Your voice, your tone

Co-written, non-clinical prompts can point to a real course or session. A reply or interaction becomes a logged continuity signal. Your voice, your tone, your service — not a generic system message pretending to be from you.

No clinical responsibility added

RCE is not being asked to make clinical decisions. It is being asked to be a real, trusted next step in the continuity thread — which it already is in practice.

In OTOS terms, RCE is one of the best existing places the continuity thread can point to while people wait. You already do this. OTOS just makes it visible and receipted.

What OTOS never does

  • No new clinical record to maintain
  • No diagnosis, prescribing or clinical decision-making
  • No EPR write-back or NHS record access
  • No commitment to full rollout by joining the scoping call

Questions to resolve together

  • Which courses or sessions work best as continuity next steps?
  • What wording feels right for messages sent in your name?
  • Which two or three of your sessions would be the best fit?
  • Who needs to be in the scoping conversation from your team?
