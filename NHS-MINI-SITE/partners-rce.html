What this means for RCE

Your courses become part of the pathway.
Officially. Visibly. Without extra work.

OTOS doesn't ask RCE to become a clinical service. It asks RCE to be exactly what it already is — and makes that visible, receipted and connected to the wider journey for the first time.

01 — Your courses become a receipted touchpoint

Visible in the continuity thread

CPFT already directs people to RCE while they wait for assessment. OTOS formalises that thread — making course attendance a receipted continuity signal, not just an invisible act of community goodwill. Your work gets seen.

02 — Course promotion through check-in prompts

OTOS points people to your timetable

Between appointments and during waiting periods, OTOS micro check-ins can point participants directly toward relevant RCE courses. You gain reach you don't currently have — without any extra marketing effort from your small team.

03 — Input into digital check-ins

Your voice, your tone

RCE's co-produced, lived-experience approach is exactly what OTOS check-ins should feel like. We'd like your input into how those prompts are written — so they reflect the warmth and peer-led quality that makes RCE courses work.

04 — Zero new admin

OTOS logs the touchpoint. Not you.

RCE has a small team and thin capacity. We know that. Nothing here adds to your admin. OTOS logs the continuity signal. You run your courses. The thread stays visible without you having to maintain it.

RCE already offers ADHD courses

RCE's timetable already includes "Living well with ADHD" — which means the people OTOS is trying to hold during their waiting period are already exactly the people RCE is designed to support. This isn't a new direction for RCE. It's a formalisation of what's already happening.

OTOS does not turn RCE into a clinical service. It simply makes the holding period visible, supported and less likely to disappear between silos.

Connected to CPFT lifestyle medicine

RCE sits within CPFT and shares its ethos with the Trust's lifestyle medicine approach — the six pillars of prevention, connection and behaviour change that run alongside clinical treatment. OTOS is designed to complement exactly that approach: keeping people connected, active and supported while the clinical pathway catches up. The pre-pilot is 50 people, 12 weeks. We are not trying to solve the whole system. We are trying to prove that holding people better during the wait changes outcomes.

What community holding looks like when the system has run out

The gap between services is where people get lost.

RCE was where I went when the clinical pathway had nothing left to offer immediately. I was referred, I was waiting, I had no keyworker and no contact from the ADHD team. RCE held me. The Facilitator got it immediately — the ADHD piece, the addiction piece, how they interact.

RCE was not treatment. It was somewhere to stay connected while everything else was still unresolved. That matters more than the clinical pathway ever acknowledged.

That kind of holding is not visible in any clinical record. It is not receipted anywhere. It exists in the memory of the person and the practitioner and when either moves on, the continuity disappears with them.

OTOS makes that holding visible. Not by recording what happens in sessions — that stays private. But by making the thread legible: this person is engaged, here, with this support, as part of this wider pathway.

"Community support is often the only thing holding a person in the system during the wait. OTOS makes that visible — so the next service knows it happened."
What OTOS makes visible
  • Active community engagement during waiting periods
  • Thread between RCE and clinical services — receipted, not assumed
  • Continuity of support across transitions — so the next service isn't starting from zero
No NHS record access needed

RCE is a VCSE community partner. OTOS is designed specifically to work across VCSE and NHS contexts — without requiring NHS record access, system integration, or clinical governance infrastructure that community organisations don't have.

What OTOS needs from RCE

Three things. None of them clinical.

Named contact

One person at RCE

A named contact to help shape how OTOS connects with RCE's community support offer — and to confirm what RCE is and isn't comfortable with in terms of thread visibility.

Thread entry point

Engagement made visible

When someone engaging with RCE is part of the pre-pilot cohort, a lightweight signal that they're engaged — no session content, no clinical information, just a receipted thread entry.

Scoping conversation

Thirty minutes together

Map what RCE does, agree the limits of what OTOS makes visible and establish a community-appropriate way of participating in the pre-pilot.

What OTOS will never do
  • ✕Access or share session content or personal support records
  • ✕Require NHS record access or clinical system integration
  • ✕Make clinical or safeguarding decisions — those stay with RCE
  • ✕Change how RCE community support is delivered
  • ✕Identify participants to other services without consent
