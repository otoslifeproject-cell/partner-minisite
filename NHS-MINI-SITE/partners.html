Shared partner briefing

The 12-week Cambridge pre-pilot.
What it asks of you.

OTOS Continuity™ is non-clinical continuity infrastructure with governed message-delivery and micro-check-in capability. It sits around existing services so the person’s journey can be held between handoffs, waiting lists, discharge points, recovery support and crisis risk.

This page is the shared read-first briefing for partners. Your individual page then explains your specific role, the time being asked, what you do not need to do, and where your input can reduce burden later.

What we ask Partner routes
Plain English

Two services. One person.
One continuity thread.

CGL, CRS, CPFT, Addenbrooke’s, RCE, GPs, RTC/QbTest, Mind, WorkWell and therapy partners all touch different parts of the same person’s journey. OTOS does not merge their records. It creates a governed tokenised thread so the handoff itself becomes visible.

What partners can see
Continuity status, touchpoints, handoff receipts, agreed next steps, drift signals and whether micro-check-ins are still being answered.
What partners do not see
Another service’s full clinical notes, complete patient record, private therapy content or clinical decision-making. Records stay separate.
OTOS surfaces signals. The partner teams make the decisions.

The pre-pilot is not a general public rollout. It is a tactical, bounded test focused on a costly cohort already touching multiple services and repeatedly falling between them.

OTOS modelling estimates that between 800 and 900 adults in the Cambridgeshire and Peterborough footprint may sit within the target ADHD-and-addiction risk profile. This is a planning estimate based on published addiction-treatment denominators, ADHD prevalence assumptions and increased addiction-risk modelling — not a CPFT-reported figure.

What we ask

Minimal ask.
Maximum visibility.

We know your time matters. We are asking for input now so the future system reduces burden later. The immediate ask is not procurement, public endorsement or clinical redesign. It is to help pressure-test the route.

Named contact
One named contact per participating organisation. Answers quick workflow questions, confirms local red lines and helps shape the pre-pilot route.
Human reviewer
Reviews drift or escalation items when surfaced, records a decision, and closes the item. Burden is measured and treated as a pre-pilot success criterion.
Support statement
Where appropriate, a short non-committal note confirming that the gap is real, the concept is worth testing, or the partner is willing to join a scoping conversation.

Micro-check-ins

A signal between human contacts.
Not a replacement for care.

The micro-check-in layer is deliberately simple: one low-friction question, repeated at agreed points, giving a basic continuity signal without requiring a keyworker to pick up the phone every time. It is not clinical monitoring and it does not replace human care.

That matters because frontline teams are already stretched. If a keyworker can only call once every two weeks, a lightweight check-in can still provide a small but useful signal between human contacts. Repetition, habit-stacking and familiar partner-written messages can support engagement without turning OTOS into a behavioural-monitoring app.

The point is not to watch people. The point is to keep the pathway warm enough that people do not disappear unnoticed.
