Shared partner briefing

The 12-week Cambridge pre-pilot. What it asks of you.

OTOS Continuity™ is non-clinical continuity infrastructure with governed message-delivery and micro-check-in capability. It sits around existing services so a person’s journey can be held between handoffs, waiting lists, discharge points, recovery support and crisis risk.

This is the shared read-first briefing for partners. Your individual page then explains your specific role, the time being asked, what you do not need to do, and where your input can reduce burden later.

What we ask Partner routes

The immediate ask

A small amount of time now. A clearer system later.

We know your time matters. We are asking for input now so the future system reduces burden later. The pre-pilot is deliberately bounded: 12 weeks, up to 50 people, human control throughout, and no replacement of existing clinical systems.

Named contact

One named contact per participating organisation to answer quick workflow questions, confirm local red lines and help shape the safest route.

Human reviewer

Drift or escalation items are reviewed by a human. The reviewer records the decision and closes the loop. If the burden is too much, we change the model.

Scoping conversation

A short conversation to confirm whether the route is practical, what needs governance, and what would make participation easier.

“This is not a request to change your service. It is a request to test whether continuity — made visible, receipted and governable — can reduce drift between services.”

Clarity

What OTOS Continuity™ is. And what it is not.

OTOS is the continuity layer around the pathway. The person can remain anonymous. Clinical notes stay where they are. The continuity signal travels.

What it creates

  • Visible handoffs between services.
  • Receipted next steps, so teams can see whether the handoff landed.
  • Earlier drift signals when silence or missed follow-through begins.
  • Micro-check-ins where partners agree the content and route.
  • A continuity audit trail that can be reviewed, measured and improved.

What it does not do

  • It is not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • It does not diagnose, prescribe or make clinical decisions.
  • It does not require full record-sharing by default.
  • It is not surveillance or another silo.
  • It does not replace safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.

The network

All the support already exists. OTOS connects it.

The pre-pilot is not trying to invent a new service. It is testing whether a single continuity thread can make existing services easier to coordinate around one person’s journey.

The cohort

Tactical first. Then scalable.

The pre-pilot begins as a surgical intervention: find a high-cost, underserved group already touching multiple services, then test whether a visible continuity thread can help people move faster and more safely toward the right support.

The cohort should be agreed between the ADHD Team, CGL, CRS and relevant clinical/service leads. It may include people who are diagnosed, suspected, waiting, stuck between steps, recently assessed, or already known to services but not moving safely through the pathway.

The point is not to create a broad frontline intake on day one. The point is to prove that a carefully selected group can be held more clearly across services, with every transition logged, receipted and reviewed.

Once proven, the model can become a wider frontline entry point. For now, the request is smaller: help us identify the right 50 people, pressure-test the route, and show whether the continuity gap can be closed.

How it works

Four steps. One connected pathway.

OTOS does not replace existing services. It connects them and maintains the person’s thread through every organisation they touch.

1

Identify

Screen and identify people in addiction, recovery, crisis or waiting-list pathways who show indicators of co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse. OTOS does not diagnose — it surfaces the signal.

2

Connect

Warm-transfer people to appropriate NHS and partner routes — ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, mental health support or recovery support.

3

Co-ordinate

Hold the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status, not another service’s clinical notes.

4

Sustain

Support appropriate follow-through, medication routes where clinically indicated, recovery support, safe re-engagement and measurable outcomes.

Signals, not noise

Micro-check-ins can show life, safety and engagement.

A simple check-in is not treatment. But one answered question can show that someone is alive, safe and still interacting — without requiring a keyworker to pick up the phone every time.

Partners can help shape the wording, rhythm and route of these messages. Used carefully, they support habit stacking, repeated engagement and early continuity signals while the person is waiting for the next step.

Safety and governance

Clinical decisions stay with clinical teams. Always.

Tokenised by default

The person can be held on an anonymised journey token. NHS number use is not required by default and would need additional governance agreement.

No clinical record replacement

OTOS does not replace EPRs, merge records or create a new clinical record. It records continuity signals and transition receipts.

Human control

Drift signals are surfaced for human review. Partner teams decide what happens next. OTOS does not auto-discharge, auto-refer or decide care.
