01
The Shared Model

Existing services. Existing routes.
One continuity layer.

OTOS is not proposing a new clinical service. It is a layer that sits around the services that already exist — making the pathway between them visible and accountable.

One person
The person may be touching addiction, ADHD, hospital, GP, charity, recovery, employment and therapy support at different points.
One continuity thread
OTOS holds the continuity signal across those touchpoints without requiring merged records or shared clinical notes.
One human-review model
OTOS surfaces signals. Partner teams make the decisions. Clinical responsibility stays with the relevant service.

We know your time matters. We are asking for input now so the future system reduces burden later.

03
The Cohort Conversation

The cohort is not selected by OTOS alone.
It must be shaped with partners.

The first step is a short joint scoping conversation with the relevant services — likely including CGL / CRS and the CPFT ADHD Team — to agree what a safe, practical, high-value pre-pilot cohort would look like.

The purpose is not to create new clinical responsibility or ask one service to take ownership of another service’s pathway. The purpose is to identify a small group of people already touching the system, where improved continuity could be tested safely and meaningfully.

For CGL / CRS
This means helping identify people already visible in addiction or recovery services who may be repeatedly drifting, relapsing, disengaging, or presenting with indicators of ADHD-related pathway need.
For the CPFT ADHD Team
This means helping define what is clinically appropriate, what is out of scope, and how any ADHD-related route should be framed without adding unsafe or unrealistic burden to the service.

We are not asking partners to solve the whole pathway in one meeting. We are asking them to help define the safest first cohort, the practical boundaries, and the human-review process for a bounded 50-person demonstrator.

04
What We Ask From Partners

Minimal ask.
Maximum visibility.

We are not asking partners to host, fund, procure or change clinical practice at this stage. We are asking for practical input, a named contact, and honest feedback on what would work safely.

Named contact
One named contact per participating organisation. Answers quick workflow questions, confirms local red lines and helps route the right people into the conversation.
Human reviewer
Reviews drift or escalation items if surfaced in the pilot, records a decision, and closes the item. Burden is measured and treated as a success criterion.
Support note
Where appropriate, a short non-committal written note of support or willingness to continue scoping the route.

If this fits the gap your service already sees, the immediate ask is simple: help us pressure-test the route, confirm what would be practical, and provide a short note of support where appropriate.
