Shared partner briefing

The 12-week Cambridge pre-pilot.
What it asks of you.

OTOS Continuity™ is non-clinical continuity infrastructure with governed message-delivery and micro-check-in capability. It sits around existing services so the person’s journey can be held between handoffs, waiting lists, discharge points, recovery support and crisis risk.

This page is the shared read-first briefing. Your individual page then explains your specific role, the time being asked, what you do not need to do, and where your input can reduce burden later.

What we ask Partner routes
The shared ask

Three things.
Small enough to start. Important enough to matter.

Named contact

One named contact per participating organisation to answer quick workflow questions, confirm local red lines and help shape the safest route.

Human reviewer

Review drift or escalation items when surfaced, record a decision, and close the loop. If the burden is too much, we change the model.

OTOS pilot lead

OTOS runs the pilot rhythm, produces weekly snapshot updates, and maintains a “what we heard / what we changed” log.

“We are not asking the system to change. Only to connect.”
