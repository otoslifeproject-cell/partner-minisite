The loop that needs breaking — and what OTOS does about it

The triangle that traps people.
OTOS breaks one side of it.

For adults with co-occurring ADHD and addiction, there is a clinical catch-22 that nobody has formally solved. OTOS doesn't solve it either — but it makes the urgent signal visible so the right clinician can.

The trap — without OTOS
ADHD untreated
Addiction continues as self-medication. CGL can't progress until addiction is "sorted."
Mental health won't engage
Services won't engage while the person is still using. But the person is still using because ADHD is untreated.
The loop repeats
Crisis. A&E. CGL. Detox. Discharge. Silence. Relapse. Crisis again. Nobody breaks the loop.
The ADHD Fast Track flag breaks one side of this triangle — at the CGL intake moment, before the loop starts again.
How the ADHD fast-track flag works

When a CGL keyworker suspects or learns of ADHD at the intake moment — right there, in that room — OTOS carries an urgent signal into the pathway. The signal is then reviewed by the appropriate clinician who decides the fastest safe route. That might be:

Route A

Back to the person's GP to make the Right to Choose request — with a receipted signal that it was flagged and when.

Route B

CPFT ADHD Team picks it up as a high-risk referral from CGL and fast-tracks internally — potentially via QbTest assessment.

Route C

Direct to Right to Choose — with OTOS receipting each step so the person doesn't fall out of contact before assessment is booked.

OTOS does not decide the route. It surfaces the signal and receipts whatever route the clinician chooses. The clinical pathway stays with the clinicians — always.

01 — Signal doesn't get lost

The urgent flag lands somewhere visible

Right now, a keyworker's hunch about ADHD gets noted in a case file and forgotten when they go off shift. OTOS makes that signal receipted, visible and actionable — by whoever is the right person to act on it.

02 — Referral is receipted

You know it was made — and whether it landed

When an RTC referral is made, OTOS logs it. The referring service knows it happened. If the person drops off before assessment is booked, that silence surfaces as a drift signal — not a mystery.

03 — No GP workload added

OTOS sits alongside the existing process

GPs make referrals the way they always have. OTOS doesn't change that. It just makes the thread visible around it — so the referral doesn't vanish into silence after it's sent.

04 — QbTech at the end of the thread

Objective assessment when the person finally gets there

QbTest is in 79 NHS trusts and NICE-recommended. It's likely already available in this pathway. OTOS makes sure people reach it — by holding the thread between referral and assessment, not by changing how QbTest works.

OTOS receipts the journey to assessment. It does not assess, diagnose, prescribe, recommend medication, influence shared-care decisions or write into any clinical record. Touchpoints, not records.

No GP workload added

OTOS does not add to the GP referral process. It sits alongside it — making the thread visible from the moment referral is made, without requiring a single additional clinical step.

What OTOS needs from GP / RTC / QbTech

Three things. None of them clinical.

Named contact

One person per pathway

A named contact at GP network level, RTC pathway and QbTech — to help design the handoff protocol and confirm what visibility is appropriate at each stage.

Referral thread

RTC referral as receipt

When a GP makes an RTC referral for someone in the pre-pilot cohort, OTOS logs it as a receipted thread step. No extra clinical action required — just visibility that it happened.

Scoping conversation

Thirty minutes together

Map the RTC pathway, agree what OTOS makes visible and what stays private, and design the lightest-possible touch for GP participation.

What OTOS will never do
  • ✕Access or write to any GP clinical system or EPR

  • ✕Add clinical workload to any GP practice
