Three roles — one continuity thread

Different entry points.
Same missing receipt.

GP, RTC and QbTech each touch a different part of the ADHD assessment pathway. OTOS sits around all three — making the handoffs visible and receipted without adding clinical burden to any of them.

If you're a GP

When you signpost to RTC, OTOS logs the signpost and flags back to the practice if no assessment booking is made within an agreed window. The referral doesn't vanish. You know it landed — or that it didn't.

If you're an RTC pathway lead

OTOS receipts the referral at your end and confirms acknowledgement back to the referring service. The handoff is visible on both sides. No silent gaps between referral sent and assessment booked.

If you're a QbTech contact

The QbTest pathway sits naturally within an RTC-enabled route. OTOS holds the continuity thread around the waiting period — so drift is visible before it becomes disengagement. Your tool does its job. OTOS holds the before and after.

The Right to Choose pathway already exists. OTOS makes sure people reach it — and that the referral doesn't disappear into silence after it's made.

What OTOS never does

  • No diagnosis, prescribing or clinical decision-making
  • No new clinical record to maintain
  • No EPR write-back or shared-care influence
  • No commitment to full rollout by joining the scoping call

Questions to resolve together

  • Which RTC and QbTest steps are appropriate to make visible?
  • What shared-care questions need addressing before the pilot?
  • Who in the GP / RTC system needs to be in the scoping conversation?
  • What clinical leadership boundaries need to be explicit from day one?
