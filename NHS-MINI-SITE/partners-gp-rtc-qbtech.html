How each route fits

Existing routes. Clearer follow-through.
Nothing clinical moves into OTOS.

Right to Choose (RTC)

The practical route by which eligible participants may access faster ADHD assessment pathways, where clinically appropriate and governed. OTOS receipts the referral so it doesn't vanish.

QbTest

An objective assessment support tool that may sit within an RTC-enabled pathway. OTOS holds the continuity thread around the waiting period — so drift is visible before it becomes disengagement.

GP signposting

GP signposting is the practical route to scale. OTOS sits around the gap between the signpost and the landed next step — receipting the handoff and flagging silence for human review.

Clear boundaries

What OTOS will never do.
And what stays entirely with you.

Guardrails — always

  • No diagnosis, prescribing or clinical decision-making by OTOS.
  • No new clinical record to maintain.
  • No EPR write-back.
  • No commitment to a full rollout by joining the scoping conversation.

Questions to resolve together

  • Which RTC and QbTest steps are appropriate to make visible?
  • What shared-care questions need to be addressed before the pilot?
  • Who in the GP / RTC system needs to be in the scoping conversation?
  • What clinical leadership boundaries need to be explicit from day one?
