The public health angle

This is not a new cost.
It is smarter use of what you already spend.

Drug and alcohol commissioning budgets are under sustained pressure. A significant portion of current spend is reactive — crisis presentations, repeat engagement, revolving-door hospitalisation and people who never address the root cause of their addiction.

Reactive & fragmented

A&E presentations, crisis mental health, repeat addiction treatment episodes, police and custody involvement, housing and homelessness support. Each service sees only its piece — and bills accordingly.

Connected & preventive

One identification route, one coordinated pathway, ADHD assessment where clinically indicated, supported recovery, fewer crisis episodes and reduced spend across multiple budget lines simultaneously.

Where the pathway leaks

The cost accumulates at transitions. The gap is between services, not within them. Upstream is where the speed needs to be.

The bounded use case

12 weeks. 50 adults. ADHD and substance misuse pathway. Cambridgeshire and Peterborough. Clear start and end conditions. Not a commitment to scale.

The decision gate

Does receipted continuity reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step?

Hospital to community

Where discharge meets follow-through.

The 10 Year Health Plan direction is hospital to community, analogue to digital, reactive to preventive. OTOS tests that transition safely.

The local ask

Five things.
Starting with a conversation.

  • Guidance on budget alignment — where existing drug and alcohol commissioning spend could hold the model, even partially.
  • Introductions to the right people — ICB commissioning leads, NHS colleagues, VCSE partners and governance routes.
  • Endorsement or support in principle — enough to unlock a properly governed scoping conversation.
  • Data intelligence — any local mapping relevant to the identified cohort that could sharpen pilot targeting.
  • A route to formal commissioning — the longer-term aim is a permanently funded, joined-up ADHD-addiction pathway.
