This is a discovery briefing — not a funding ask. OTOS Continuity™ is a bounded pre-pilot demonstrator targeting adults in Cambridgeshire cycling through addiction, crisis and fragmented support, where unresolved ADHD may be the underlying cause of their addiction and the driver of repeat demand.

This page is for Local Authority level — county-wide public health, not the district council and not the ICB. The purpose is discovery: does OTOS fit what you see as a priority gap in the Cambridgeshire drugs and alcohol pathway, and is there a route worth exploring together?

Hospital to community

Where discharge meets follow-through.

The 10 Year Health Plan direction is hospital to community, analogue to digital, reactive to preventive. OTOS tests that transition safely — starting at the earliest possible point of contact with services.

The public health angle

This is not a new cost.
It is smarter use of what you already spend.

Drug and alcohol commissioning budgets are under sustained pressure. A significant portion of current spend is reactive — crisis presentations, repeat engagement, revolving-door hospitalisation — without ever addressing the root cause.

Reactive & fragmented

A&E presentations, crisis mental health, repeat addiction treatment episodes, police and custody involvement, housing and homelessness support. Each service sees only its piece — and bills accordingly. The root cause goes untreated.

Connected & preventive

One identification route, one coordinated pathway, ADHD assessment where clinically indicated, supported recovery, fewer crisis episodes and reduced spend across multiple budget lines simultaneously.

The ripple cost

The cost doesn't disappear.
It just moves to a different budget line.

Adults with undiagnosed or unresolved ADHD cycle through A&E, housing, probation, detox and recovery while the pathway treats the symptom that arrives first. If the underlying condition remains out of reach, the cost does not disappear — it moves.

Where the pathway leaks

The cost accumulates at transitions. The gap is between services, not within them. Upstream identification and receipted follow-through is where the speed needs to be.

The bounded use case

12 weeks. 50 adults. ADHD and substance misuse pathway. Cambridgeshire and Peterborough. Clear start and end conditions. A specific, answerable question — not a commitment to scale.

The decision gate

Does receipted continuity reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step? The pre-pilot generates the evidence to answer that question locally.

The local ask — Cambridgeshire County Council

Three questions.
Starting with a conversation.

We are not asking you to commission anything. We are asking three questions: does this problem match what you see locally in Cambridgeshire? Does this approach make sense as a response? And if so — what is the right route to explore it further?

  • Does this match what you see locally? — Is co-occurring ADHD and addiction a visible gap in the Cambridgeshire drugs and alcohol pathway? Do the people we are describing show up in your data?
  • What is the right route? — Public Health commissioning, ICB innovation, joint sponsorship, VCSE partnership, or another practical path. What is the cleanest route for a 12-week demonstrator in Cambridgeshire?
  • Who else needs to be in the room? — What is the minimum set of decision-makers who need to be involved to make a properly scoped pre-pilot real in this county?
  • Guidance on budget alignment — where existing Cambridgeshire drug and alcohol commissioning spend could hold or part-hold the model without requiring new budget.
  • A route to formal commissioning — the longer-term aim is a permanently funded, joined-up ADHD-addiction pathway in Cambridgeshire. The pre-pilot is the evidence base that makes that case to the ICB.
