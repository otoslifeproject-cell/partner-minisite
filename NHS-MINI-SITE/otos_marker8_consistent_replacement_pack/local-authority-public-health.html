The public health angle

This is not a new cost.
It is smarter use of what you already spend.

Drug and alcohol commissioning budgets are under sustained pressure. A significant portion of current spend is reactive — crisis presentations, repeat engagement, revolving-door hospitalisation and people who never address the root cause driving the addiction.

Current spend pattern

Reactive and fragmented. A&E presentations, crisis mental health, repeat addiction treatment episodes, housing and homelessness support. Each service sees only its piece.

With OTOS Continuity

Connected and preventive. One identification route, one continuity thread, one governed way to see whether the next step happened.

This is briefing material Scott can forward upward: clear, bounded, honest about assumptions, and framed around local authority and ICB governance.

1. Where the pathway leaks

The cost accumulates at transitions. The gap is between services, not within them.

2. The bounded use case

12 weeks. 50 adults. ADHD and substance misuse pathway. Clear start and end conditions. Not a commitment to scale.

3. The decision gate

Does receipted continuity reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful step?

Hospital to community

Where discharge meets follow-through.

The 10 Year Health Plan direction is hospital to community, analogue to digital, reactive to preventive. OTOS tests that transition safely.

The local ask

Five things.
Starting with a conversation.

  • Guidance on budget alignment — where existing drug and alcohol commissioning spend could hold the model, even partially.
  • Introductions to the right people — ICB commissioning leads, NHS colleagues, VCSE partners and governance routes.
  • Endorsement or support in principle — enough to unlock a properly governed scoping conversation.
  • Data intelligence — any local mapping relevant to the identified cohort that could sharpen pilot targeting.
  • A route to formal commissioning — the longer-term aim is a permanently funded, joined-up ADHD-addiction pathway.
