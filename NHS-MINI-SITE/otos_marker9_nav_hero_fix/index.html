The problem

Hidden in plain sight.
ADHD is driving addiction.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

What it is

A continuity layer.
Not another clinical system.

OTOS sits around existing services. It does not replace records, diagnose, prescribe or decide care. It creates a safe, tokenised thread so the right teams can see whether the journey is holding.

What it helps partners achieve

  • People are less likely to vanish between handoffs because the next step is receipted.
  • Waiting-list risk becomes visible earlier, before silence becomes crisis.
  • Teams can see whether support is connecting without opening another service’s clinical notes.
  • Partner-written messages and micro-check-ins can create safe signs of life between contacts.
  • Commissioners get evidence they can trace, govern and improve.

What it is not

  • Not an EPR, clinical record or diagnostic platform.
  • Not a prescribing, treatment or clinical decision-support system.
  • Not surveillance, and not full record-sharing by default.
  • Not another service asking frontline teams to start again.
  • Not a replacement for safeguarding, crisis or existing partner protocols.
How it works

Four steps.
One connected pathway.

OTOS connects existing services and maintains the person’s continuity thread through each organisation they touch. The person can remain anonymous. Clinical notes stay where they are. The continuity signal travels.

01

Identify

Screen and identify people in addiction, recovery, crisis or waiting-list pathways where co-occurring ADHD and substance misuse may be present.

02

Connect

Warm-transfer to appropriate NHS and partner routes — ADHD assessment, Right to Choose, CPFT, GP, mental health or recovery support.

03

Co-ordinate

Hold the continuity thread across services so each partner can see the relevant continuity status, not another service’s clinical notes.

04

Sustain

Support follow-through, medication routes where clinically indicated, recovery support, safe re-engagement and measurable outcomes.
