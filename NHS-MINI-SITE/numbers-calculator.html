Model discipline

Observed, modelled, proxy, assumption.
Never mixed quietly.

The current best position is honest: adult addiction treatment data are stronger than adult ADHD service data. The geography also does not map perfectly. Addiction data is often Local Authority based, while ADHD service data is NHS provider or national-proxy based.

Label Meaning How it is used
Published source A figure from a published research paper, government source, NHS source or official dataset. Used as evidence or comparator, with source retained in the evidence register.
Local source A local service figure or partner-confirmed number. Used only where the local source is recorded and can be validated.
OTOS modelling estimate A calculated planning figure based on published denominators and assumptions. Used to size the opportunity, not as an observed fact.
Assumption A scenario value used to test sensitivity. Must remain visible until replaced by local evidence.

Scenario evidence

Why 25% is a scenario.
Not a hidden claim.

Research on ADHD prevalence in substance-use populations varies widely by country, setting, substance type and diagnostic method. A 25% mid-case is defensible as a modelling scenario because it aligns with alcohol-subgroup pooled findings and sits close to multiple comparator studies. It remains an assumption until local data validates or replaces it.

Scenario Value Support Strength
Low 5% Low-end study prevalence around 5.22% in extracted research table. Weak but directly supported.
Mid 25% Matches pooled alcohol subgroup and sits near several study rows around 25%. Moderate as a modelling scenario.
High 65% Supported by a small clinic sample showing 65.5% persistent adult ADHD in multiple substance addiction. Weak / setting-specific.
