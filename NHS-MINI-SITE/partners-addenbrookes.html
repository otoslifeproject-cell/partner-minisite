Addenbrooke's — Alcohol Care Team

She said:
"I have nowhere else to send you."

The last time I was at Addenbrooke's as a patient, my alcohol liaison nurse sat bedside and told me she had no onward pathway to offer. She was brilliant. She cared. But the system had nothing left to give her to give to me.

So I gave her my words. I told her what I needed the discharge letter to say — and she wrote it exactly as I asked, so I could use it to get back to my private psychiatrist and finally get on Elvanse.

Within three days, everything changed. Within hours of the first dose, my whole life made sense. The chaos, the addiction, the shame, the repeated hospitalisations — they weren't failures. They were what happens when the underlying neurological condition stays unresolved and the system treats only what arrives first.

I told her: "Next time we meet, I won't be your patient. I'll be your partner."

This is me keeping that promise.

What OTOS gives every clinician like her

Somewhere to send them.
With a receipt that it landed.

The moment someone leaves the ward is the moment the thread most often snaps. OTOS logs that handoff before discharge — visible, receipted, and held until the next step is confirmed.

The bedside moment

Mobile-first. Under 60 seconds. Register touchpoint, select onward route, issue handoff receipt. Done before the person reaches the car park.

The receipted handoff

CGL, CPFT, GP, RCE — whoever is the next step. The handoff is logged and visible to both sides until it is confirmed as landed. Silence becomes a signal, not a blind spot.

The follow-through

If the next step goes quiet, OTOS flags it for human review. A person makes the decision. The person doesn't disappear back into the system without a trace.

"I have nowhere else to send you" — should never be the end of the conversation. OTOS gives it a next step.

Clear boundaries — always

What OTOS will never do.
And what stays entirely with your team.

No new clinical record

OTOS logs a continuity signal — not a clinical event. No new record is created. Nothing writes back to any EPR system. The clinical record stays exactly where it is.

No diagnosis or prescribing

OTOS does not diagnose, prescribe, recommend treatment or make any clinical decision. Every action requires human review and human decision. Always.

No EPR write-back

OTOS is not integrated with any hospital or NHS record system. It is a separate, governed continuity layer. The clinical record stays where it belongs.
