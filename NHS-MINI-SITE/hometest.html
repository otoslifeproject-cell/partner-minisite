01
The Simple Explanation

OTOS does not replace services.
It holds the thread between them.

The care already exists. The buildings exist. The teams exist. The problem is the space between them: referrals that are not receipted, discharges that go quiet, waiting-list support that is not connected, and people who disappear from view until they reappear in crisis.

OTOS Continuity™ is a non-clinical continuity infrastructure layer.

It logs touchpoints, creates transition receipts, surfaces drift signals and maintains a timestamped continuity trail. It does not diagnose, prescribe, replace clinical judgement, or become another clinical record. Partner teams make the decisions. Always.

02
The Problem

When ADHD is left waiting,
addiction can become the coping system.
Then the whole system pays.

Many people are cycling through addiction services, mental health crisis, hospital discharge, post-rehab relapse risk and community support while the underlying driver remains unsupported. What looks like a behaviour problem can be a continuity problem: the person is not held long enough, clearly enough, or consistently enough to reach the next meaningful step.

34%
Median ADHD prevalence in addiction treatment cohorts
Royal College of Psychiatrists research gives a 23–45% range. ADHD is significantly overrepresented in addiction services and often missed at the point of entry.
£47bn
Annual UK economic cost of alcohol and drugs
Reactive spend is repeated when the system treats visible symptoms without reliably holding people through the underlying pathway.
877

61.3%
Improvement comparator after ADHD diagnosis
START study evidence provides a strong comparator for the pre-pilot: what happens when the underlying ADHD driver is properly addressed?
03
The Solution

The missing layer is not another service.
It is continuity.

OTOS sits around existing services and makes follow-through visible. It shows whether the handoff happened, whether the next step was receipted, whether someone has drifted, and whether a human reviewer needs to look again.

It logs touchpoints
Every important contact, referral, discharge, support step or handoff can become part of a visible continuity thread.
It creates receipts
The system can see whether the next step happened, stalled, or disappeared into silence.
It keeps humans in control
OTOS surfaces signals. Partner teams review and decide. It does not diagnose, prescribe or automate clinical decisions.
04
How It Works

Five steps.
Clear enough for any partner to follow.

1
A touchpoint happens
A person is seen, referred, discharged, signposted, checked in, or handed over.
2
The thread is held
The touchpoint is logged against a tokenised continuity journey, not a new clinical record.
3
The handoff is receipted
The next step is acknowledged, unresolved, or waiting for review. Silence becomes visible.
4
Drift is surfaced early
Missed follow-through becomes a drift signal before it turns into crisis or relapse.
5
A human decides
OTOS surfaces the signal. Partner teams decide what happens next. Every time.

07
Why This Matters Beyond One Service

Whole-person care needs more than one record.
Especially when charities are part of the journey.

The NHS and public-sector direction is clear: join up care, reduce repetition, and treat the whole person. But many of the services that actually hold people day to day — charities, recovery groups, employment support, therapy and peer networks — may never have access to a full NHS patient record.

Records stay separate
OTOS does not require every organisation to see everything. It holds continuity signals around the journey while records remain where they belong.
Support becomes visible
A support course, therapy touchpoint, recovery contact or employment step can help hold the thread without becoming a clinical record.
The person stops repeating themselves
The system can see follow-through, not private notes. That is enough to reduce drift, duplication and silence between services.
