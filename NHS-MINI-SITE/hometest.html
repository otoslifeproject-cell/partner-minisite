01
What OTOS Is

A safe bridge between services.
Not another system on top of them.

OTOS is not a new support service, clinical service, diagnostic platform, prescribing system or patient-record replacement.

It is the continuity infrastructure around the route: touchpoint logging, handoff receipts, drift signals, audit trail and shared visibility — so existing partners can move a stuck person faster toward the ADHD-related step that may unlock the rest of the pathway.

Secure and tokenised
The person can remain anonymous by default. OTOS holds a continuity signal, not a shared clinical record.
Built around real services
Each partner sees the part of the journey they need — without seeing everyone else’s notes.
Designed to prevent drift
Missed follow-through, silent disengagement and broken handoffs become visible early enough for human review.

OTOS unites the silos at the continuity layer only.

No merged EPRs. No shared clinical notes. No diagnostic claims. No EPR write-back by default. Human decisions only.

02
How It Works

Existing services. Existing routes.
One continuity layer.

A worker logs a touchpoint. The next step is named. The handoff is receipted. If the person goes quiet, drift becomes visible for human review.

OTOS does not press the RTC, QbTest, diagnostic or medication button. OTOS makes the need, handoff and follow-through visible. The authorised clinical or service partner presses the button under agreed governance.

03
The Problem

Hidden in plain sight.
ADHD is driving addiction.

Thousands of people are cycling through addiction services, mental health crisis, homelessness and revolving-door hospitalisation — while the underlying cause remains undiagnosed, untreated or under-held.

For a significant and identifiable group, the first meaningful step is not more generic support. It is getting them to the right ADHD-related route: assessment, diagnosis, medication review, titration, treatment access, shared care or holding support while waiting.

34%
Median ADHD prevalence in addiction cohorts
Royal College of Psychiatrists research gives a 23–45% range. ADHD is significantly overrepresented in addiction services.
£47bn
Annual UK economic cost of alcohol and drugs
Reactive spend repeats when visible symptoms are treated without moving people toward the unresolved driver.
877

61.3%
Improvement comparator

ADHD and addiction do not fail in one service. They fail between them.

The Pre-Pilot

50 people.
A surgical proof point.

The pre-pilot starts as a tactical intervention into a costly, stuck cohort: people repeatedly cycling through addiction, crisis, hospital, recovery or mental-health touchpoints where unresolved ADHD may be keeping the pattern alive.

The question is whether partners can identify this cohort, activate the right ADHD-related pathway faster, and use OTOS to keep the journey visible enough that people do not disappear between services.
