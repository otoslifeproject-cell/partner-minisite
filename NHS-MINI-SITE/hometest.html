01
What OTOS Is

A safe bridge between services.
Not another system on top of them.

OTOS Continuity™ sits quietly around existing services. It does not replace CGL, CPFT, GPs, hospitals, recovery services, charities, therapy, or community support.

It creates one secure, anonymous continuity thread around the person’s journey, so authorised teams can see whether a contact happened, whether a handoff landed, and whether silence is becoming risk.

Secure and tokenised
The person can remain anonymous by default. OTOS holds a continuity signal, not a shared clinical record.
Built around real services
Addiction, ADHD, hospital, GP, recovery, charity and therapy partners can each see the part of the journey they need — without seeing everyone else’s notes.
Designed to prevent drift
Missed follow-through, silent disengagement and broken handoffs become visible early enough for a human team to review.

In simple terms: OTOS helps services hold the thread.

Addiction teams, ADHD teams, hospital liaison, GPs, recovery services, charities and support workers can make better, earlier, human decisions — without needing one shared clinical record.

The Problem

When ADHD is left waiting,
addiction can become the coping system.
Then the whole system pays.

Many people are cycling through addiction services, mental health crisis, hospital discharge, post-rehab relapse risk and community support while the underlying driver remains unsupported. What looks like a behaviour problem can be a continuity problem: the person is not held long enough, clearly enough, or consistently enough to reach the next meaningful step.

34%

Royal College of Psychiatrists research gives a 23–45% range. ADHD is significantly overrepresented in addiction services and often missed at the point of entry.
£47bn

Reactive spend is repeated when the system treats visible symptoms without reliably holding people through the underlying pathway.

05
The Question Underneath

The system often sees the addiction first.
But what if ADHD is the driver?

Addiction is often the visible crisis. For some people, that is the primary condition. But for a significant and identifiable group of adults, the drinking or drug use may be what arrives in view while unsupported ADHD remains underneath.

That matters because self-medication can appear to work — until it does not. Alcohol or substances can briefly do what an untreated ADHD brain has been trying to do all along: supply motivation, sustain attention, create focus, cut the noise. The trouble starts when that coping strategy becomes dependency, relapse risk and crisis.

The visible problem gets treated
Services quite reasonably respond to the addiction, crisis, discharge, relapse risk or missed appointment that appears in front of them.
The underlying driver can stay untouched
If ADHD remains unsupported, the work may not hold. Motivation, planning, recall, sequencing and follow-through are exactly where the person may already be struggling.
Waiting-list support becomes critical
This is where RCE-style courses, ADHD-informed content, micro check-ins and community support can help hold the person while the clinical pathway moves.

07
How OTOS Works

One touchpoint becomes a thread.
One thread makes the journey visible.

OTOS sits lightly around the services that already exist. When a person is seen at the bedside, in recovery, by a GP, by a support worker, or while waiting for ADHD care, that moment can be logged as a simple continuity touchpoint.

The person can remain tokenised. The records stay separate. OTOS does not expose clinical notes, merge systems, diagnose, prescribe, or decide care. It simply shows whether the next step happened, whether the handoff landed, and whether silence is becoming risk.

That gives each partner a clearer view of the part they already play — and gives the whole pathway a way to see follow-through before someone disappears from view.

1
A touchpoint is logged
At the bedside, in the hub, in recovery, in primary care or in community support, a worker records that contact happened. It takes seconds and does not create a new clinical record.
2
The next step is named
The handoff, referral, support offer or follow-up is linked to the same tokenised journey. The system now knows what should happen next.
3
Follow-through is receipted
OTOS shows whether the next step was acknowledged, completed, unresolved or still waiting. It answers the question most systems cannot: did the handoff actually land?
4
Drift becomes visible
If the journey goes quiet, the signal is surfaced for human review. OTOS does not act clinically. It gives the right partner team a chance to decide what happens next.
For frontline staff
One tap to log a touchpoint. One glance to see whether the next step happened. No new clinical record, no EPR write-back, no extra paperwork by default.
For service partners
A clearer view of the journey across CGL, CRS, CPFT, RCE, Addenbrooke’s, GPs, RTC, QbTest, Mind, WorkWell and therapy support — without forcing everyone into one shared record.
For commissioners and funders
A measurable continuity trail: handoffs, receipts, drift signals, partner burden and outcome movement. Evidence that can be reviewed, challenged and improved.

Why This Matters Beyond One Service

Whole-person care needs more than one record.
Especially when charities are part of the journey.

The NHS and public-sector direction is clear: join up care, reduce repetition, and treat the whole person. But many of the services that actually hold people day to day — charities, recovery groups, employment support, therapy and peer networks — may never have access to a full NHS patient record.

Records stay separate
OTOS does not require every organisation to see everything. It holds continuity signals around the journey while records remain where they belong.
Support becomes visible
A support course, therapy touchpoint, recovery contact or employment step can help hold the thread without becoming a clinical record.
The person stops repeating themselves
The system can see follow-through, not private notes. That is enough to reduce drift, duplication and silence between services.
