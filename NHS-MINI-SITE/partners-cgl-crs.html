The route in practice

From silence to signal.
Human review stays in control.

A keyworker logs a touchpoint or handoff. The next step is receipted, acknowledged or marked unresolved. If silence becomes a drift signal, the agreed human reviewer sees it and decides what happens next.

Touchpoints

  • Existing work is logged in seconds.
  • No new clinical record is created.

Receipts

  • The handoff becomes visible.
  • Partners can see whether the next step landed.

Drift signals

  • Silence becomes visible earlier.
  • A human decides the safe next step.
