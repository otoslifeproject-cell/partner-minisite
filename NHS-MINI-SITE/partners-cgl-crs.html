What this means for your team

Seven things that get easier.
Nothing that gets harder.

OTOS wraps around what CGL and CRS already do — from crisis intake and structured recovery work through to community recovery. Nothing changes in how you work. What changes is what you can see.

01 — No more chasing

Your client stays connected between appointments

Micro check-ins create a quiet continuity signal between formal contacts. If someone goes quiet, the drift is visible before it becomes another crisis presentation or a lost recovery opportunity.

02 — Every referral receipted

You know it landed

Every agreed next step can be logged as received, pending or unresolved. CGL does not have to wonder what happened after a referral, handoff or signpost left the room.

03 — Drift signals

You're told who needs attention

OTOS does not diagnose risk. It surfaces silence. A named human can see when contact has cooled, follow-through has not happened, or the thread needs a human check.

04 — ADHD route visible

Suspected ADHD no longer disappears into memory

Where ADHD may be driving repeat relapse, crisis or disengagement, the agreed next step can be logged and tracked. OTOS does not assess or prescribe. It keeps the route visible.

05 — Whole journey visible

No more starting from scratch

When someone returns through CGL, CRS, GP, ADHD, community or crisis routes, the continuity picture can show where the last known touchpoint was — not the clinical detail, just the thread.

06 — CGL to CRS handoff held

The community recovery move becomes visible

CRS is not a side note. It is where recovery keeps living after structured treatment. OTOS helps make the move from CGL into CRS visible, receipted and less dependent on memory, inboxes or individual availability.

07 — Community touchpoints count

Recovery support is seen, not assumed

Recovery cafés, groups, wellbeing activity and peer-led support can become visible continuity touchpoints. The session content stays private. The fact that the person is still connected does not disappear.

How the continuity thread works

A touchpoint happens. The thread is held. Follow-through becomes visible. Drift is surfaced early. The partner team decides what happens next. OTOS surfaces the continuity picture; people make the decisions.

CGL already recognises ADHD in addiction

CGL has already published work linked to the development of an ADHD assessment pathway within substance-use services — including a named pathway in Nottinghamshire. OTOS does not duplicate that assessment role. It solves a different problem: continuity between addiction, ADHD, community recovery and the agreed next step.

CGL has already recognised ADHD inside addiction services. OTOS adds the missing continuity between services.

A focused pre-pilot — not a list solution

We are not trying to rush the whole waiting list or redesign recovery services. We want to test a bounded continuity layer around 50 people over 12 weeks — adults already visible to CGL, CRS and partner services, where co-occurring ADHD may be driving repeat addiction, crisis or disengagement.

What OTOS changes for addiction and recovery services

The work is already happening. The thread is not always visible.

People arrive at CGL and CRS at very different points: intake, relapse, aftercare, community recovery, or after a period of silence. The support may be there, but the continuity between moments is often held in people, inboxes and memory.

OTOS makes that holding visible and receipted — so the next person has continuity without depending on a specific worker being available at the right moment.

You've seen what happens when the thread doesn't hold. People cycle back. The same crisis. The same intake form. The same question: "have you been with us before?" You already know the answer.

What OTOS would change
  • Intake becomes a receipted entry point into the continuity thread
  • Handoff into community recovery is visible before contact is lost
  • Waiting-list or aftercare silence triggers a continuity signal, not a dropped case
No new burden

OTOS does not add clinical burden. It does not require case-management access. Keyworker involvement is the 30-minute scoping call — after that, OTOS runs alongside your existing work.

CRS / Community recovery

Recovery does not end when treatment pauses.
CRS is where continuity must hold.

Cambridgeshire Recovery Service is the community recovery part of the pathway. It is where people stay connected through recovery cafés, peer support, wellbeing activity, creative recovery work and local community groups.

That matters because the risk does not always arrive during a formal appointment. It often arrives in the space after treatment, between contacts, during a quiet week, or when someone has stopped appearing but has not yet visibly relapsed.

OTOS fits around CRS by making those community touchpoints part of the continuity thread. It does not record session content. It does not turn CRS into a clinical service. It simply makes the fact of connection visible, so the wider pathway can see that recovery is still being held.

"CGL may begin the recovery turn. CRS helps it hold in the community. OTOS makes that continuity visible between them."

Why this matters now: the NHS direction of travel is from hospital to community, from analogue to digital, and from sickness to prevention. OTOS does not claim NHS endorsement. It simply sits in the same direction: earlier visibility, safer handoffs and more continuity around community support.

What this gives CRS
  • Community recovery touchpoints become visible without sharing private group or session content
  • Someone leaving CGL does not have to restart the story when they enter CRS support
  • Silence between contacts becomes a continuity signal, not an invisible gap
No new clinical burden

CRS keeps doing community recovery. OTOS only adds a visible continuity thread around agreed touchpoints, handoffs and follow-through. The partner team remains the decision-maker.

What OTOS needs from CGL / CRS

Three things. None of them clinical.

Named contact

One person across CGL / CRS

A named contact to answer workflow questions, confirm local boundaries and help shape the continuity protocol from CGL intake into CRS community recovery.

Entry point visibility

Intake and aftercare as thread points

When someone presenting with ADHD-relevant risk enters CGL, moves toward CRS, or reconnects in community recovery, the thread can be logged. Not a clinical decision — just visible continuity.

Scoping conversation

Thirty minutes together

Map the pathway, agree the boundaries, design the safest route in. No commitment required beyond that conversation.

What OTOS will never do
  • ✕Access or interface with Caspian or any case management system
  • ✕Create clinical records or share identifiable information
  • ✕Add keyworker workload beyond the initial scoping call
  • ✕Make clinical decisions or automate care decisions
  • ✕Replace any existing CGL/CRS safeguarding or crisis protocol
