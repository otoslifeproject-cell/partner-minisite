What this means for your team

Seven things that get easier.
Nothing that gets harder.

OTOS wraps around what CGL and CRS already do — from first contact and structured support through to community recovery. It does not change your work. It makes the handoff, the silence, the suspected ADHD next step, community recovery and all CPFT touchpoints on Continuity, visible.

01 — No more chasing

Your client stays connected between appointments

Micro check-ins via SMS create a quiet continuity signal between formal contacts. If someone goes quiet, the drift is visible before it becomes another crisis presentation or a lost recovery opportunity.

02 — Every referral receipted

You know it landed

Every agreed next step can be logged as received, pending or unresolved. CGL does not have to wonder what happened after a referral, handoff or signpost left the room.

03 — Drift signals

You're told who needs attention

OTOS does not diagnose risk. It surfaces silence. A named human can see when contact has cooled, follow-through has not happened or the thread needs a human check.

04 — ADHD Fast Track

Suspected ADHD has a visible next step

CGL or CRS can refer out to the ADHD Fast Track within Continuity when ADHD is suspected. Not a diagnosis, not prescribing and not clinical ownership — just a fast, receipted route so the person can be guided toward the right assessment pathway.

05 — Whole journey visible

No more starting from scratch

When someone returns through CGL, CRS, GP, ADHD, community or crisis routes, the continuity picture can show where the last known touchpoint was — not the clinical detail, just the thread.

06 — CGL to CRS handoff held

The community recovery move becomes visible

CRS is not a side note. It is where recovery keeps living after structured treatment. OTOS helps make the move from CGL into CRS visible to CPFT, receipted and less dependent on memory, inboxes or individual availability.

07 — Community touchpoints count

Recovery support is seen, not assumed

Recovery cafés, groups, wellbeing activity and peer-led support can become visible continuity touchpoints. The session content stays private. The fact that the person is still connected does not disappear.

CGL already moved first

CGL Nottingham has already shown that ADHD belongs inside the addiction conversation. OTOS does not duplicate assessment, diagnosis or prescribing. This is why the scoping conversation should be easy to start. We are not asking CGL or CRS to become an ADHD diagnostic service. We are asking whether a bounded continuity layer can make the agreed next step visible, receipted and less likely to disappear.

What OTOS changes for addiction and recovery services

The work is already happening. The thread is not always visible.

People arrive at CGL and CRS at very different points: intake, relapse, aftercare, community recovery, or after a period of silence. The support may be there, but the continuity between moments is often held in people, inboxes and memory.

OTOS makes that holding visible and receipted — so the next person has continuity without depending on a specific worker being available at the right moment.

When the thread does not hold, people cycle back. The same crisis. The same intake form. The same question: "have you been with us before?" You already know the answer.

"Continuity™ doesn't replace your keyworkers. It makes sure the thread they picked up doesn't get dropped when they're not in the room."

What I'm asking: not for CGL or CRS to change how they work. Just to let OTOS wrap around what you already do — so the next-step referral is visible, receipted and trackable across the whole pathway.

What OTOS would change
  • Intake becomes a receipted entry point into the continuity thread
  • Handoff is logged — visible to the next service before contact is lost
  • Suspected ADHD can be routed into the ADHD Fast Track rather than disappearing into notes or memory
  • Waiting-list silence triggers a continuity signal, not a dropped case
No new burden

Continuity™ does not add clinical burden. It does not require case management access. Keyworker involvement is the 30-minute scoping call — after that, OTOS runs alongside your existing work.

CRS / Community recovery

Recovery does not end when treatment pauses.
CRS is where continuity must hold.

Cambridgeshire Recovery Service is the community recovery layer: Recovery Cafés, peer connection, wellbeing activity, creative recovery and local community groups. This is where people stay connected after structured treatment, during fragile transitions, or when formal contact has reduced.

OTOS makes that community holding visible without turning CRS into a clinical service. A recovery café, community touchpoint or follow-up can be part of the continuity thread without sharing private session content or creating a new patient record.

This is also where the NHS direction of travel matters. The move from hospital to community only works if handoffs into community support are visible enough to hold. OTOS helps show that the person has not disappeared; they are still connected somewhere in the pathway.

"CGL may begin the recovery turn. CRS helps it hold in the community. OTOS makes that continuity visible between them."
What this gives CRS
  • Community recovery touchpoints become visible without sharing private group or session content
  • Someone leaving CGL does not have to restart the story when they enter CRS support
  • Silence between contacts becomes a continuity signal, not an invisible gap
What OTOS needs from CGL / CRS

Three things. None of them clinical.

Named contact

One person across CGL / CRS

A named contact to answer workflow questions, confirm local boundaries and help shape the continuity protocol from CGL intake into CRS community recovery.

Entry point visibility

Intake, aftercare and ADHD Fast Track

When someone presenting with ADHD-relevant risk enters CGL, moves toward CRS, or reconnects in community recovery, the thread can be logged. Not a clinical decision — just visible continuity and a receipted next step.

Scoping conversation

Thirty minutes together

Map the pathway, agree the boundaries, design the safest route in. No commitment required beyond that conversation.
