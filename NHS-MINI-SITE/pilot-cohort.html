Pilot Cohort Model

A surgical intervention.
A bounded proof point.

The pre-pilot starts tactically: identify a costly, stuck cohort already cycling through addiction, crisis, hospital, recovery or mental-health touchpoints where unresolved ADHD may be keeping the pattern alive.

If this works as a surgical intervention, the same continuity model can later become a frontline entry point.

01
Who This Is For

A high-cost cohort.
Already visible, but still stuck.

The cohort is not selected by OTOS alone. The first cohort should be agreed through a short joint scoping conversation between the services closest to the risk — initially CGL / CRS and the CPFT ADHD Team, with other partners added where the route requires them.

The purpose is to identify a small, safe, high-value group of people already visible to the system, where continuity is most likely to break: addiction/recovery, ADHD assessment or medication review, hospital discharge, GP/RTC route, community support, or re-engagement after silence.

Already visible to services
People already touching addiction, recovery, hospital, ADHD, GP, VCSE or community support routes.
Still trapped in the loop
People repeatedly relapsing, disengaging, missing handoffs, waiting without support, or cycling between services.
Suitable for a bounded test
People where a continuity thread can be tested without changing clinical responsibility or forcing unsafe pathway decisions.

The aim is not to create another support service. The aim is to help existing partners identify a costly, stuck cohort and move them faster toward the ADHD-related step that may unlock the rest of the pathway.

02
Why ADHD + Addiction First

Treating the addiction alone
may not unlock the pattern.

The system usually treats addiction as the primary condition. For some people, that is right. For a significant and identifiable group of adults, it is not. The primary condition is ADHD. The drinking or drug use is what arrives in view.

That matters because self-medication can work — until it does not. Alcohol or substances can briefly do what an untreated ADHD brain has been trying to do all along: supply motivation, sustain attention, create focus and cut the noise. The trouble starts when that coping strategy turns into dependency and crisis.

At that point, services quite reasonably respond to the addiction. But if the ADHD remains untouched, the work may not hold. Motivation strategies meet an executive function that was never the problem.

The pre-pilot question is simple: if partners can identify this cohort and activate the right ADHD pathway faster, while OTOS keeps the person visible between services, does the pattern of addiction, drift, relapse and crisis begin to change?

03
The ADHD-Related Step

The route may be different.
The continuity need is the same.

The ambition is to identify up to 50 people where ADHD assessment, diagnosis, medication review, treatment access or continuity failure may be the key unresolved step.

Some people may need assessment. Some may already be diagnosed but stuck waiting for medication review, titration or shared care. Some may have only just reached the point where diagnosis or treatment becomes possible. Some may be suspected by the services already working with them.

Assessment / diagnosis route
Where ADHD is suspected and partners agree a clinically governed route is appropriate.
Medication review / titration route
Where ADHD is already diagnosed but the person is stuck waiting, drifting, relapsing or repeatedly re-presenting.
Holding support while waiting
Where RCE, VCSE, recovery, therapy or community support can help hold the person while the clinical pathway moves.

OTOS does not press the RTC, QbTest, diagnostic or medication button. OTOS makes the need, handoff and follow-through visible. The authorised clinical or service partner presses the button under agreed governance.

04
First Wave

Start small enough to govern.
Then expand toward 50.

The preferred operational starting point is a hand-selected first wave of approximately 10–12 participants, agreed with partners, to test the workflow safely before scaling toward the full 50-person demonstrator.

Where clinically appropriate and operationally feasible, the first wave should include people already known to relevant services and where the next step is clear enough to test continuity quickly.

The first wave may include people who are:
Already diagnosed with ADHD; suspected of ADHD by a keyworker or clinician; waiting for ADHD assessment; awaiting medication review or titration; known to addiction/recovery services and repeatedly drifting, relapsing or disengaging.
This is not an exclusion rule
It is a practical way to generate clean early evidence while the wider route is designed safely. The pilot can still include people who are diagnosed, suspected, waiting, medicated, under-reviewed, or stuck between steps.
05
Partner Boundaries

This needs both sides in the room.
But not to take over each other’s work.

For CGL / CRS, the question is: who is already visible in addiction or recovery services where ADHD-related pathway need, relapse risk, drift or disengagement may be part of the pattern?

For the CPFT ADHD Team, the question is: what is clinically appropriate, what is out of scope, and how should any ADHD-related route be framed without adding unsafe or unrealistic burden?

What CGL / CRS helps define
Addiction/recovery visibility, relapse risk, engagement patterns, practical keyworker touchpoints, and where the current pathway goes silent.
What CPFT ADHD helps define
ADHD pathway reality, safe framing, clinical boundaries, route language, appropriate criteria, and what should not be promised or implied.

We are not asking partners to solve the whole pathway in one meeting. We are asking them to help define the safest first cohort, the practical boundaries, and the human-review process for a bounded demonstrator.

06
Referral and Consent Pathway

Plain English.
Governed consent.

OTOS may include participant-facing features such as consented check-ins or SMS prompts, but these are infrastructure features used under partner governance. OTOS does not become the care provider, keyworker, clinician or crisis service.

1. Identify
A named worker or clinician at a participating service identifies a person who may fit the agreed cohort criteria.
2. Explain
The worker introduces OTOS using agreed plain-English framing: “a continuity check-in that stays with you between services.”
3. Consent
If the person agrees, consent is completed using the agreed paper or digital process.
4. Tokenise
The person is onboarded with a tokenised journey ID. No NHS number is required by default unless additional governance is agreed.
5. Start thread
The first touchpoint is logged and the continuity thread begins.
6. Surface drift
If the thread starts to break, OTOS surfaces a drift signal for human review. The partner team decides what happens next.
07
What The Demonstrator Tests

Not everything.
One clear question.

Does a receipted continuity thread, applied to this cohort, measurably reduce silent disengagement and increase the likelihood that people reach the next meaningful ADHD-related step?

Continuity KPIs
Follow-through visibility, receipt completion rate, drift-detection lead time, silent disengagement made visible earlier, and median time between touchpoints.
Burden & safety KPIs
Partner time spent per week, human-review loop performance, safe escalation discipline, and no new clinical burden introduced.
Participant KPIs
Whether people reach the next step, whether the pathway holds for longer, and whether services can see more of what happened.
08
What Is Out Of Scope

Clear boundaries.
No accidental clinical creep.

OTOS does not diagnose
ADHD diagnosis, clinical assessment and treatment decisions remain with the appropriate clinical pathway.
OTOS does not prescribe
Medication decisions, titration and shared-care arrangements remain clinical decisions under existing governance.
OTOS does not merge records
Partner records remain separate. OTOS holds continuity signals, receipts and audit trail only.
OTOS does not replace services
CGL, CPFT, CRS, GP, RCE, VCSE and therapy partners remain the services. OTOS connects the gaps between them.
OTOS does not automate care
The system surfaces drift signals. A human reviewer decides what happens next.
OTOS does not promise pathway capacity
The pre-pilot tests continuity visibility and follow-through, not a guaranteed acceleration of all clinical routes.
