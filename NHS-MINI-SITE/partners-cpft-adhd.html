I'm still on your list.
This is what I built while I waited.

My medication review still hasn't come through. I went private because I couldn't wait — and within hours of getting Elvanse, everything made sense. The chaos. The addiction. The shame. All of it finally had a frame.

I know you are under enormous pressure. The CPFT data is published — 6,000 in triage, 380 waiting for assessment, around 1,000 awaiting medication review. OTOS is not designed to solve that. It is designed to ensure people don't fall through the gaps while they wait for you.

The continuity layer holds the thread: referral logged, wait visible, drift detected, human review triggered. Your team makes every decision. Nothing clinical moves into OTOS.

What we need from CPFT ADHD

Hold the pathway between steps.
Without changing clinical ownership.

OTOS is not proposing to diagnose ADHD. It connects people to existing ADHD routes and support options — and keeps the thread visible while they wait.

Existing ADHD routes

OTOS connects people to existing ADHD assessment routes including Right to Choose. It does not create a new clinical service or ask keyworkers to become diagnosticians.

Waiting-list continuity

The continuity layer keeps the whole-person journey visible across referral, medication review, handoff and follow-through — so silence doesn't become disengagement.

Evidence the handoff landed

A referral is a signpost. A handoff receipt is a connection. OTOS provides the receipt. Your team stays in control of every clinical decision.

Clear boundaries

What OTOS will never do.
And what stays entirely with you.

Guardrails — always

  • No new clinical record to maintain.
  • No diagnosis, prescribing or clinical decision-making by OTOS.
  • No EPR write-back.
  • No commitment to a full rollout by joining the scoping conversation.

Questions to resolve together

  • Who in the ADHD team needs to be in the scoping conversation?
  • What touchpoints are realistic around your current pathway?
  • What would a support note look like that stays within your governance?
  • How does this interact with shared care and RTC routes?
