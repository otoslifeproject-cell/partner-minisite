Claims discipline

Published. Local. Modelled. Placeholder.
The label matters.

Any number used in the portal must be labelled. If a source is missing, the page should say so. If a figure is modelled, it stays modelled until replaced by local validation.

Claim type Meaning How it appears
Published source Research, NHS, government, OHID, NICE, RCPsych, or official dataset. Can be used as evidence, with caveat where needed.
Local source Local service figure, partner figure, or local JSNA / public-health figure. Used only when the local source is recorded.
OTOS modelling estimate Calculated planning estimate based on source figures and assumptions. Never described as observed or identified.
Placeholder requiring validation Useful claim or figure not yet source-locked. Flagged until validated or removed.
Core evidence

ADHD and addiction.
The link is not new.

The current evidence base supports the direction of travel: ADHD is overrepresented in substance-use populations; treating ADHD in people with co-occurring substance use can improve ADHD and substance-use outcomes; and failure to hold continuity after discharge or referral creates avoidable risk.

Evidence item Figure / finding Label Notes
Rohner et al. meta-analysis 21% pooled overall ADHD prevalence among SUD populations; subgroup estimates include 25% alcohol. Published source Used to support the scenario range, not as a local observed figure.
RCPsych / ADHD with substance use 23–45% range used in current working copy. Published source Needs exact citation line locked in final claims register.
START study 61.3% improvement in addictive disorders at six months after ADHD diagnosis. Published source Comparator only. Not identical to 12-month re-presentation reduction.
NICE NG87 NICE guidance covers ADHD recognition, diagnosis and management, including coexisting substance misuse context. Published source Useful for alignment, not a direct OTOS endorsement.
CGL Nottingham precedent Trialled ADHD identification and referral pathway in Nottingham in 2024–25. Local / partner precedent Useful as “not from scratch” evidence, but needs final wording/source locked.
