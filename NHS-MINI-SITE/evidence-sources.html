Royal College of Psychiatrists
✓ Published

23–45% of people in addiction treatment have undiagnosed ADHD

RCPsych research shows ADHD is significantly overrepresented in addiction cohorts — and consistently missed at point of entry into substance misuse services. The range reflects variation across substance types and service contexts. The direction is consistent: ADHD is a major underrecognised driver in this population.

ADHDAddictionPrevalence
START Study
✓ Published

61.3% improvement in addictive disorders at 6 months after ADHD diagnosis

The START study provides the strongest available directional evidence that addressing the underlying ADHD driver changes the downstream addiction picture materially. Not a marginal signal. This is the comparator figure used in the OTOS pre-pilot measurement framework.

START StudyOutcomesPre-pilot baseline
NHS England
✓ Published

608,416 alcohol-dependent adults in England (2019/20)

Published NHS England figure. For a substantial subset of this cohort, addiction is not the whole clinical picture. When ADHD remains unrecognised or untreated, the conditions required for behavioural change are weaker — attention, impulse control, working memory, task initiation and follow-through are all compromised.

Turning Point / London School of Economics
✓ Published

£4.07 return for every £1 invested in better addiction continuity

Evidence from Turning Point and the LSE. OTOS is designed to generate the locally validated evidence that makes this ROI case defensible in a Cambridge commissioning context.

HM Government

Published government figure. OTOS does not claim to address this in full. The pre-pilot is designed to test whether continuity infrastructure can reduce a specific, identifiable subset of avoidable demand within the CPFT footprint.
